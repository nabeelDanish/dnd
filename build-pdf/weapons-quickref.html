Weapons

Quick reference · A4 landscape · v2

The roll

Attack = d20 + primary + training (if trained) vs. ARMOR Damage = weapon die + material + primary
  • Your one primary ability powers every attack — weapon and spell. Training → the hit; material → the damage.
  • Crit (nat 20): double the dice; flat mods once. Fumble (nat 1): miss + a complication.

Weight rules

  • Set to Strike: on a turn you move and attack, a heavy weapon or heavy armor → that attack is at disadvantage; both → move or attack, not both. Stand still = strike normally.
  • No ranged weapons in heavy armor.
  • Dual-wield (two light): die steps up one — 1d4→1d6, 1d6→1d8. Both hands (no shield), still one attack. = medium damage, mobile + throwable.

Remember

  • Material → damage only, never the attack roll (keeps accuracy bounded at d20+9).
  • Material is mundane masterwork, not magic; the material is the quality tier.
  • Light = flexible sidearm / dual-skirmisher · Medium = shield + solid damage · Heavy = max damage, planted.

Worked examples

  • Knight, Might +5, Steel greatsword (heavy): atk +8 → 1d10 +1 +5 (planted).
  • Duelist dual-wielding shortswords: 1d6 →1d8 +1 +primary, mobile, no shield.
  • Ranger, Agi +5, Steel longbow: 1d8 +1 +5 — free to move & shoot (light armor).
