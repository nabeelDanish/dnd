# Pre-Made Characters

Four **level-3** characters, ready to play *The Pale Gate*. One of each class. Each is built a little tough — *The Pale Gate* is a five-fight gauntlet with one optional rest, so every sheet leans into survivability and into the two things that hurt the Hollow most: **radiant** and **fire**, and the one thing that keeps you effective in it: **resisting fear.**

**Level-3 notes:** training bonus is `+2` (apprentice tier). Each character has their two level-1 signature features **plus one level-3 feature.** Casters know **4 spells + 2 cantrips** and have `3 + ability` mana. No ability bump until level 4.

**How to use:** print or copy a sheet for each player. Personality and hook sections are roleplaying suggestions, not rules.

> **Optional continuity:** these four can be played as the *same heroes* from [The Bell of Ashvane](../the-bell-of-ashvane/adventure.md), two levels on and now working out of Bridghaven — or as a fresh party. Either way they share that adventure's world: the **Sea-Watcher**, the light-keeping faith, matters here too.

## 1. Brakka Iron-Gut — Orc Warrior

> *A slab of a woman in dented half-plate, a greataxe slung like a walking stick. She has a scar for every winter and a laugh you can hear two rooms away. She is the one who walks into the dark first, on purpose.*

### Stats
- **Might** `+5` *(base +3, +1 Warrior, +1 Orc)*
- **Agility** `+2`
- **Spirit** `+1`
- **Mind** `+0`

### Vitals
- **HP:** 30
- **Armor:** 16 *(10 + 1 Agility (capped, medium) + 3 chain shirt + 2 shield)*
- **Mind defense:** 10
- **Spirit defense:** 11
- **Speed:** 6 squares

### Trainings (`+2` bonus)
- **Athletics** *(Warrior)*
- **Tactics** *(Warrior)*
- **Intimidation** *(background: gate-warden)*

### Class features
- **Cleave.** When your attack reduces a creature to 0 HP, immediately make another attack as part of the same action against a different target within reach. *(Brutal against the husk swarms.)*
- **Bulwark.** While you wear medium or heavy armor, allies adjacent to you have `+1` to their Armor defense.
- **Second Wind** *(level 3).* Once per fight, as your action, regain `1d6 + Might` HP (`1d6 + 5`).

### Ancestry trait
- **Savage Reserves** *(Orc).* Once per rest, when you would drop to 0 HP, you instead drop to `1` HP. *(Your "do not die in the boss fight" button — save it.)*

### Attacks
- **Greataxe** (heavy, Might): **`+7` vs. Armor → `1d10 + 5`**. *(d20 + 5 Might + 2 training.)*
- **Handaxe** (light, Might): **`+7` vs. Armor → `1d6 + 5`**. Range Close, or thrown to Near.

### Gear
- Chain shirt + shield (worn).
- Greataxe, two handaxes.
- A torch and a tin of pitch (you like to set things on fire; out here, that's wisdom).
- Adventuring kit.
- A child's wooden soldier, painted, carried since you found it in a sacked village years ago and never found whose it was.
- 25 gold.

### Background — Gate-warden of Bridghaven
You spent six years on the night-gate of Bridghaven, the one job nobody wanted: standing in the dark, deciding who got let in. You're good at the dark. You're good at standing between it and people who can't fight. The reeve trusts you, which is why you got first call on the Hollowmere job.

### Personality
- **Loves:** a good fire, loud songs, being the wall.
- **Fears:** that one day you'll be the one who needs carrying, and there'll be no one big enough.
- **Hides:** the wooden soldier. You'd never admit you still look for whose it was.

### Hook into the adventure
The reeve handed *you* the Hollowmere contract personally. Sixty people walked into the dark and didn't come back. You have spent your whole life being the thing that stands between the dark and people like that. **This one is personal before you've even arrived.**

## 2. Nix — Goblin Scout

> *A small, quick goblin in patched grey, a longbow worn smooth with use, eyes that have already counted the exits. Talks fast, moves faster, trusts almost no one — and yet keeps signing up to walk into terrible places for people who'll never thank a goblin.*

### Stats
- **Agility** `+5` *(base +3, +1 Scout, +1 Goblin)*
- **Mind** `+2`
- **Might** `+1`
- **Spirit** `+0`

### Vitals
- **HP:** 17
- **Armor:** 16 *(10 + 5 Agility + 1 leather)*
- **Mind defense:** 12
- **Spirit defense:** 10
- **Speed:** **8 squares** *(Goblin)*

### Trainings (`+2` bonus)
- **Stealth** *(Scout)*
- **Perception** *(Scout)*
- **Wilderness** *(background: moor-poacher)*

### Class features
- **Sneak Strike.** Once per turn, when you attack with advantage (or with an ally adjacent to your target), deal an extra `1d6` on a hit.
- **Slip Away.** When an enemy adjacent to you takes any action that isn't attacking you, you may move up to half your speed without provoking.
- **Deadeye** *(level 3).* On a turn where you do not move, your ranged attack deals an extra `1d6` damage on a hit. *(Stack with Sneak Strike for big single-target hits — great against Hollow Knights and the Shepherd.)*

### Ancestry trait
- **Scurry** *(Goblin).* Your speed is `8` squares. You can move through the spaces of creatures larger than you. *(You will never be the one the Wax-bound corners.)*

### Attacks
- **Longbow** (ranged, Agility): **`+7` vs. Armor → `1d8 + 5`**, Range Far. *(d20 + 5 Agility + 2 training.)*
  - With Deadeye (didn't move) + Sneak Strike: `1d8 + 5 + 1d6 + 1d6`.
- **Shortsword** (light, Agility): **`+7` vs. Armor → `1d6 + 5`**.
- **Thrown dagger:** `+7` vs. Armor → `1d4 + 5`, Range Near.

### Gear
- Leather armor (worn).
- Longbow + 30 arrows (including six **fire arrows** — oil-wrapped; a hit deals normal damage and sets the target **Burning** if flammable; the Hollow's things very much count).
- Shortsword, two daggers.
- Thieves' tools, a coil of black rope, a hooded lantern (you of all people keep a light handy).
- Adventuring kit.
- 14 gold.

### Background — Moor-poacher
You grew up running snares on the high moors, the only goblin for forty miles, taking the lord's hares because nobody else would feed you. You know peat bogs, fog, and how to not drown in either. You know moor villages like Hollowmere — and you know they never, ever ploughed. *(You might be the first to crack the Vault.)*

### Personality
- **Loves:** open sky, a clean shot, proving a low opinion of goblins wrong by saving the people who hold it.
- **Fears:** enclosed dark. The Hollow's fog is going to test you.
- **Hides:** that you've started to think of this rag-tag party as the closest thing to a warren you've had.

### Hook into the adventure
You've poached the moors around Hollowmere. You knew faces there — the fishwife who'd buy a hare and not ask where it came from; the kid who waved at the goblin instead of throwing stones. When you heard the whole village walked into the dark, you didn't wait to be hired. **You were already packing.**

## 3. Calla Wren — Human Mage

> *A sharp-faced young woman with soot on her cuffs and a notebook she never stops scribbling in. She studies things that frighten other scholars — the spaces between, the doors that shouldn't open. She walked toward Hollowmere when everyone else walked away.*

### Stats
- **Mind** `+5` *(base +3, +1 Mage, +1 Human)*
- **Agility** `+2`
- **Spirit** `+1`
- **Might** `+0`

### Vitals
- **HP:** 12
- **Armor:** 12 *(10 + 2 Agility, no armor)*
- **Mind defense:** 15
- **Spirit defense:** 11
- **Speed:** 6 squares
- **Mana:** **8** *(level 3 + Mind 5)*

### Trainings (`+2` bonus)
- **Arcana** *(Mage)*
- **Investigation** *(Mage)*
- **Lore** *(background: hollow-scholar)*
- **Perception** *(Human "Adaptable" trait)*

### Class features
- **Spellcasting.** You know 4 spells and 2 cantrips (below). Mana pool `8` per rest. Cantrips are free.
- **Arcane Recovery.** Once per fight, when you roll your spell attack die, you may reroll a `1`.
- **Arcane Surge** *(level 3).* Once per fight, cast one **Lesser** spell (1 mana) for **0 mana.** *(In a five-fight day with no resupply, this is gold — an extra Burning Hands or a free Shield.)*

### Ancestry trait
- **Adaptable** *(Human).* You took a fourth training (Perception, above).

### Cantrips
- **Spark.** Spell attack `+7` vs. Armor → `1d6` **fire**, Range Near. Flammable things catch. *(d20 + 5 Mind + 2 Arcana.)*
- **Daze.** Spell attack `+7` vs. **Mind** → target **Stunned** until end of its next turn, Range Near. *(A free stun on a Hollow Knight or a Wax-bound is enormous.)*

### Spells known
- **Magic Missile** (1 mana). Auto-hit. Three darts, `1d4 + 1` each, split as you like across up to three targets. Range Far. *(Reliable — no roll, ignores Flicker, finishes wounded things.)*
- **Shield** (1 mana, reaction). When hit, add `+5` Armor until your next turn; can turn a hit into a miss. *(Your survival button — you have 12 HP.)*
- **Burning Hands** (1 mana). 3-square cone, `+7` vs. Armor each → `2d6` **fire**. *(Roasts husk swarms; softens and hurts Wax-bound.)*
- **Thunderwave** (1 mana). 3-square cube, `+7` vs. Armor each → `2d8` thunder and **push 2 squares.** *(Crowd control and positioning — shove things off you, or knock a Wax-bound out of a grapple range.)*

### Attacks (non-spell)
- **Dagger** (light, Agility — not trained): **`+2` vs. Armor → `1d4 + 2`**. *(Last resort. Stay behind the Orc.)*

### Gear
- Traveling robes (no armor — armor blocks your casting).
- Dagger.
- Spellbook (chained to belt), a satchel of chalk, salt, and a candle-stub or two.
- A hooded lantern and a spare; you of everyone understand that *light is a weapon here.*
- Adventuring kit.
- 22 gold.

### Background — Hollow-scholar
You study liminal magic — thresholds, mirror-realms, the grey places forgotten things fall into. The Academy thought it morbid. You think it's the most important work there is, because *those doors keep opening and nobody else is reading them.* When you heard a whole village walked into a memorial lamp, you understood faster than anyone what it meant.

### Personality
- **Loves:** a question no one else will ask. The smell of a fresh notebook. Being *right* about something terrifying.
- **Fears:** that you'll walk through a door you can't walk back out of. (You always check for the way back. Always.)
- **Hides:** how badly you want to see the Hollow — not just survive it. Study it.

### Hook into the adventure
You have a whole notebook on the Lamplighter legend already — every version, every village that "went quiet" near a dead memorial lamp on the dark of the year. Hollowmere isn't a tragedy to you yet; it's a *confirmation.* **You may understand what the Pale Shepherd is before he even speaks** — and you're the one most likely to figure out how to put out his flame.

## 4. Hethra Bron — Dwarf Priest

> *A broad, grey-bearded dwarf in a chain shirt and a sea-blue stole, a lantern-shaped holy symbol on her chest and a mace on her hip. She is a keeper of lights — that is the whole of her faith — and she has walked a long way to keep one from going out.*

### Stats
- **Spirit** `+4` *(base +3, +1 Priest)*
- **Might** `+3` *(base +2, +1 Dwarf)*
- **Agility** `+1`
- **Mind** `+0`

### Vitals
- **HP:** 22
- **Armor:** 16 *(10 + 1 Agility + 3 chain shirt + 2 shield)*
- **Mind defense:** 10
- **Spirit defense:** 14
- **Speed:** 6 squares
- **Mana:** **7** *(level 3 + Spirit 4)*

### Trainings (`+2` bonus)
- **Religion** *(Priest)*
- **Medicine** *(Priest)*
- **Insight** *(background: Sea-Watcher acolyte)*

### Class features
- **Spellcasting.** You know 4 spells and 2 cantrips (below). Mana pool `7` per rest. Cantrips are free.
- **Divine Touch.** As your action, touch a creature: heal `1d6 + 4` HP. Free, usable **5** times per rest *(1 + Spirit)*. *(Your bread-and-butter healing — doesn't cost mana.)*
- **Channel Light** *(level 3).* Once per fight, as your action: every undead or Hollow creature within Near range must roll **Spirit vs. your spell attack** (`d20 + 4 + 2`) or be **Frightened of you for 1 round and take `1d8` radiant.** *(Turns the swarm and damages it. The husks, hounds, wisps, Wax-bound, and Knights are all "Hollow" — the Shepherd is immune to the fear but not the radiant.)*

### Ancestry trait
- **Stone-Hearted** *(Dwarf).* **Advantage on Spirit defense against fear and charm.** Darkvision 30 ft. *(The Hollow is a fear engine and pitch-dark in places — you are built for exactly this place. You may be the only one who never goes Frightened.)*

### Cantrips
- **Sacred Flame.** Spell attack `+6` vs. **Spirit** → `1d8` **radiant**, Range Near. Target glows (negates Hide/Invisible) until end of its next turn. *(d20 + 4 Spirit + 2 Religion. Radiant = the Hollow's worst nightmare; this is your at-will weapon and it hits hard against these things.)*
- **Light.** Touch an object; it sheds bright light in a 6-square radius for 1 hour. *(Strips Lantern Wisp Flicker and Hollow Knight advantage. Cast it on the Orc's shield and walk a bubble of daylight through the dark.)*

### Spells known
- **Cure Wounds** (1 mana). Touch, heal `2d8 + 4`. *(Your big heal.)*
- **Healing Word** (1 mana). Far range, heal `1d6 + 4`; wakes a downed ally. *(Save a Downed PC from across the room — critical in the wave fights.)*
- **Bless** (1 mana, ongoing). Up to three allies in Near range; each gets advantage on their next attack. *(Open a hard fight with this.)*
- **Spirit Guardians** (Greater, 3 mana, ongoing). Spectral guardians fill the squares around you; each enemy that ends its turn within 1 square takes `3d8` radiant (half on a Spirit save). *(Your swarm-melter. Walk into the husk wave with this up and they die for touching you. Radiant, so it does* extra *here — the single best spell in the party for this adventure. Costs your whole mana on a 7-pool, though; pick your fight.)*

### Attacks
- **Mace** (medium, Might): **`+5` vs. Armor → `1d8 + 3`**. *(d20 + 3 Might + 2 training.)*
- **Light crossbow** (ranged, not trained): **`+1` vs. Armor → `1d6`**, Range Far. *(You carry it. You're not good with it.)*

### Gear
- Chain shirt + shield (worn).
- Mace, light crossbow + 20 bolts.
- Lantern-shaped holy symbol of the Sea-Watcher (silver).
- **A real, well-made storm-lantern** and a flask of good oil — your order's tool and your faith's whole symbol. *Keep it lit.*
- A flask of consecrated oil (one use: throw at an undead/Hollow creature → `1d6` radiant + **Burning**).
- Adventuring kit; the *Litany of the Watchful*, your order's prayer book.
- 20 gold.

### Background — Sea-Watcher acolyte
The Sea-Watcher is the faith of kept lights — lighthouses, signal-bells, the lamps that keep travelers off the rocks and out of the bog. Your whole vocation is *the light must not go out.* You took your vows at forty (young, for a dwarf) and you've tended shrine-lamps from the coast to the inland moors for thirty years. A village that lost all its lights and all its people is, to you, the most sacred kind of emergency.

### Personality
- **Loves:** the moment a lamp catches in the dark. Strong tea. Stubborn people.
- **Fears:** arriving to a light already out. (It's happened. You carry their names.)
- **Hides:** a private terror that the dark is winning everywhere, a little at a time, and one priest with one lantern can't hold it back.

### Hook into the adventure
A village that *gathered all its lights to one place and then vanished into it* is, to a Sea-Watcher, almost scripture — the exact heresy your faith exists to stand against. You'd have come unpaid. And when you meet the Pale Shepherd — a lamplighter who decided the only way to keep a light safe was to steal it into the dark forever — **you will understand him completely, and you will refuse him utterly.** This is your fight more than anyone's.

**The party is built for this place.** Calla brings fire, Hethra brings radiant and fear-immunity, Brakka brings the wall and the "don't die" button, Nix brings mobility and single-target burst for the elites and the boss. **Protect Calla** (12 HP). **Keep Hethra's lantern lit and her standing** — she's the lifeline and the fear-anchor. Push the fire and radiant; the Hollow hates both.
