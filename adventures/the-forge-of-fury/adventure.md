# One-Shot: The Forge of Fury

A single-session adventure for **five level-4 characters**. Play time: **~4–5 hours**. Bring your own party — no pre-mades.

This is your party's first proper **dungeon crawl** — a delve into the ruined dwarven hold of **Khundrukar** under a dead mountain. Where [The Pale Gate](../the-pale-gate/adventure.md) was a fixed corridor of escalating fights, this is a *place*: five zones the party can approach loud or quiet, fast or careful, and the mountain answers accordingly. Freely adapted from the classic module of the same name — reforged for this system and dressed in the house tone: the dungeon has a broken heart at the bottom of it, and a grief that armed a war no one came to fight.

It's built for players who already know the system. Read the **Survival & pacing** and **The clock** sections before you run it — the difficulty here is not a fixed number, it's *how much noise the party makes.*

## The pitch

Two hundred years ago, the dwarven smith **Durgeddin the Black** and his clan carved a stronghold into the roots of **Stonehome Mountain**, three days north of Bridghaven. They called it **Khundrukar**. From its forge came blades so keen and so hateful they passed into legend — *Durgeddin's black steel*, forged to kill orcs and never chip on the killing. Then, one winter, the orcs found Khundrukar. They came in their thousands. When it was over, the forge was cold, the clan was ash, and the black blades were gone into the dark.

Khundrukar has changed hands a dozen times since. Right now a caravan-master named **Halvard Kess** — who lost two mule-trains and a nephew on the north road this spring — has traced the raids to the old hold: an **orc warband** has taken the upper gate, and something worse pays them tribute from below. Kess will pay the party **well** to go up the mountain, clear the raiders' nest, and — if the stories are true — bring back anything of Durgeddin's make that's still down there.

**200 gold now, 300 on return**, and first claim on whatever they haul out of the deep. Kess doesn't know what's really at the bottom of Khundrukar. Neither, yet, do you.

## The truth (GM only)

Durgeddin was a master, and a broken one. When orcs slaughtered his clan-hall at Dunhollow in his youth, he did not grieve and heal — he *swore*. He built Khundrukar not as a home but as an **armory for a vengeance**: he forged and forged, hoarding hundreds of orc-killing blades against the day he'd raise an army and burn the orc-holds off the mountains. That day never came. He forged for forty years, alone but for the last of his kin, and his hate curdled into obsession. He would not leave the hoard. He would not forgive. When the orcs finally traced the smoke of his forge, he had a mountain of weapons and no one left to wield them. They died in the dark, defending steel that killed nothing.

His shade still walks the **Foundry**, hammering a cold anvil, guarding blades for an army of ghosts. *He is the grief at the bottom of this dungeon* — not the villain, the wound. Play him the way you'd play the Pale Shepherd or Florian of the Vermilion Garden: certain, sorrowful, and wrong.

**Who holds Khundrukar now, top to bottom:**

- **The Mountain Door (upper gate):** an orc warband under **Great Ulfe**, a half-ogre brute who wears one of Durgeddin's black axes and hasn't the faintest idea what it is. They raid the north road and squat in the tomb of the people they exterminated — a fact none of them find strange.
- **The Glitterhame (natural caverns):** a clan of **troglodytes** who tolerate the orcs above and fear the thing below. They hold a prisoner: **Yorrin Ashfell**, a dwarf antiquarian who came seeking Durgeddin's forge and got as far as the caves. He is the party's living key — he knows the hold's history, its waterworks, and the dragon.
- **The Foundry (Durgeddin's forge):** relit. A crew of **duergar** — gray dwarves from deeper down — have found the forge and are trying to crack Durgeddin's metallurgy and loot his blade-cache, ferrying it out by barge along the underground river. Durgeddin's shade haunts them; his old **traps and forge-guardians** still work. The duergar pay tribute to the dragon to be left alone.
- **The Black Lake (the deep):** **Nightscale**, a young black dragon, lairs in a flooded cavern fed by the Dark Mere above. She's the "something worse" — young, greedy, cruel, and *afraid of nothing in this mountain except the one thing that ever cut her.* The duergar are her tenants. She is the boss.

**The two seeded win-buttons** (plant both; let the party earn the fight they want — see the scenes):

1. **Durgeddin's black steel bites her.** When Nightscale was a wyrmling, she blundered into Khundrukar and a black blade opened her hide to the bone — the one wound she remembers. A **Durgeddin blade** (recovered from Ulfe, the cache, or the forge) treats her thick hide as if it weren't there: **ignores her damage resistance and deals +1d6.** She *fears* the black blades — a PC brandishing one visibly rattles her.
2. **Drain the lake.** Nightscale fights amphibious: she submerges (full cover, and the party can't reach her) and surfaces to breathe acid. The dwarves built **sluice-gates** to control the Dark Mere's seep. Working the old mechanism drains the Black Lake over three rounds and **strands her on the mudflats** — no submerging, no diving heal, and she fights at **disadvantage** out of her element. The controls are in the Foundry; the clue-trail to them runs through Yorrin and the carvings.

## The shape of the session

Khundrukar is a *place*, not a corridor — but a first-time party will move down it in roughly this order. Adjust to their route.

| Beat | Type | Threat | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Combat** — the gate & the bridge | ~10 (~50%) | The Mountain Door |
| 2 | **Combat** — the chieftain's hall | ~17 (~85%) | Great Ulfe's Hall |
| 3 | **Investigation & social** — the prisoner | — | The Glitterhame |
| 3b | **Combat** — troglodyte cave (avoidable) | ~14 (~70%) | The Glitterhame |
| — | *Optional hazard — the roper in the dark* | ~6 | The Sinkhole |
| 4 | **Combat & set-piece** — the forge | ~17 (~85%) | The Foundry |
| — | *The rest / the shade* (Durgeddin) | — | The Foundry |
| 5 | **Boss** | ~18 (solo) | The Black Lake |

Budget reference: five level-4 PCs have an encounter budget of **20 threat points** (party level × players). 100% is a standard fight; ~150% is hard; a solo boss runs the full budget. See [../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md](../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md). **These are the *quiet-run* numbers.** If the party wakes the mountain (see the clock), fights get reinforcements and the boss gets nastier — that's the whole tension of the delve.

## The clock — "The Mountain Wakes"

There's no time-of-day clock here. The pressure is **noise**. Khundrukar is a chain of tenants who mostly ignore each other — until someone starts a war upstairs. The louder and slower the party is, the more the mountain rouses beneath them, and the worse the deep gets.

Run it as a **mood track with three thresholds** — you don't need exact numbers, just a sense of how loud the party has been. Advance it when the party:
- fights in the open instead of picking off / sneaking (a horn sounds, a scream carries),
- sets off a trap or alarm, triggers a rockfall, or lets an enemy flee to warn others,
- **takes the full rest** at the bolt-hole (see below) — the deep doesn't sleep while they do.

**Threshold 1 — Stirring.** The orcs above blow warhorns; **Great Ulfe's Hall (Beat 2) is on alert** — no surprise for the party there, and add **1 Orc Raider** to that fight.

**Threshold 2 — Roused.** Word reaches the Foundry. The **duergar seal the forge doors** (a Hard check or a fight to open) and start **loading the black-blade cache onto the river barge** — every further delay means fewer blades left to recover (less reward, and fewer Durgeddin blades for the boss fight). The duergar overseer will **flee to the Black Lake** if hard-pressed, becoming an add in the boss fight.

**Threshold 3 — Awake.** **Nightscale wakes and hunts.** She is now alert for the boss (no chance to catch her basking/half-asleep), begins the fight **already in the water**, and gets her **Frightful Roar** on round one. If the party has been catastrophically loud, she may even come *up* the tunnels to meet them in the Foundry — a memorable, terrifying escalation.

**The one rest — the Bolt-hole.** Behind a concealed dwarven door off the Glitterhame is a dry, defensible **bolt-hole** (an old sentry-cell). A party that barricades in gets a **full rest** — all HP, all mana, all once-per-rest abilities, conditions cleared. The only one available. **The cost:** while they sleep, the mountain wakes a full threshold (advance the clock one step, GM's choice which). **Tell them the deep won't wait.** This is the resource-drama lever from [../../rules/14-gm-guide.md](../../rules/14-gm-guide.md): the rest that saves you arms the thing that will try to kill you.

## Survival & pacing (read this)

Five zones with one optional rest is a full adventuring day. Keep it hard-but-survivable:

- **They start fresh** — full HP, mana, once-per-rest abilities. Reward the party that scouts: stealth, a Dwarf's darkvision, a *Light* cantrip held in reserve, and any means of picking enemies off quietly all *keep the clock down* and make the whole delve easier. Say so in the fiction.
- **Stealth is a real option and a real reward.** The Mountain Door can be scouted and its sentries picked off; the troglodyte cave can be *skirted entirely*; the duergar can be talked to or ambushed. A clever party fights three of these beats, not five. Don't punish avoidance — the clock is the reward for it.
- **Healing is seeded along the route**, not saved for the end: potions on the orc chief, mushrooms in the Glitterhame, the bolt-hole rest, a dwarven mercy-cache in the Foundry. Listed per scene. Don't withhold them.
- **Wounded enemies break.** Orcs are brave until Ulfe falls, then routable; troglodytes are cowards; duergar are mercenary and will parley or flee a losing fight. New enemies arriving (the clock) is fair; buffing the ones on the table is not.
- **The boss is a solo dragon — it can kill a level-4 PC in a round if the party fights it dumb.** That's why *both* win-buttons are seeded upstream. If the party found neither, that's a signal to soften Nightscale (see her block's dials), not to TPK them. If they found both, let them feel like giant-slayers.
- **A PC can die here.** Signal danger early — the road is littered with orc-raid dead, Yorrin is terrified of the deep, the black blades are wet with old dragon-blood — so any death is earned, not a gotcha.

## How to run this

You're the GM. Reference the rules ([../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md](../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md) through [../../rules/15-quickstart.md](../../rules/15-quickstart.md)) as needed. If you have an AI assistant (Claude) at the table, ask them to roll for monsters/NPCs, track combat state and the mountain-wakes clock, look up rules, read NPC dialogue (see [cast-and-dialogue.md](cast-and-dialogue.md)), and suggest monster tactics. The rulings are yours.

### The battlemap

A pre-built map is included: [map.json](map.json). It's a **vertical descent** — the surface gate at the top, the dungeon sinking downward to the Black Lake at the bottom, with the Sinkhole on an east spur. All NPC and enemy tokens are placed; **player tokens are NOT** — drop them at the **PLAYERS START** marker (the pink square on the road outside the gate).

To load it:

1. Open `map-tool/index.html` in a browser (DM view).
2. In the toolbar, click **Import map…** and pick `adventures/the-forge-of-fury/map.json`.
3. The Mountain Door sits near the top; the hold descends downward (the Black Lake is far below).
4. Click **Open Player View →** to spawn the player window; mirror it to the table's screen.
5. **Turn Fog of War ON** before play (Fog tool → "Fog of War: ON") and paint reveals as the party descends — the fog is what keeps the deep dark until they reach it.

To regenerate or tweak the map, edit `build-map.js` in this folder and run `node build-map.js`.

There are also copy-paste art prompts in [image-prompts.md](image-prompts.md) for the mountain, the cast, and the climax.

**The dungeon's texture:** it is dark (darkvision and light both matter), old, and full of dwarven work — everything the orcs squat in was built by the people they murdered. Lean on that irony. Water runs through the whole hold, downhill, toward the Black Lake.

## Scene 1 — The Mountain Door (Combat 1)

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The old dwarf-road ends at a cliff face and a great arched gate cut into the mountain's root — its doors long gone, the carved dwarven faces above it hacked and defaced. A cold stream pours out of the dark and off the ledge into a gorge. Just inside, the passage breaks: a **chasm** splits the floor, forty feet down to black water, and a single **rope-and-plank bridge** sags across it. Two orcs lounge at the far side around a low fire, throwing bones. A third dozes against a pillar carved like a screaming dwarf. Somewhere deeper, a horn hangs on the wall — for calling the rest.*

The warband's outer guard. This is the **teaching fight** and the party's first real choice about *how loud they want this to be.*

**The quiet way (reward it):** The sentries are bored and inattentive. A party that scouts can pick them off — a ranged opener with surprise, a thrown-voice distraction, cutting the horn-line first. If the party kills or silences all three **before any can reach the horn**, the mountain stays asleep (don't advance the clock) and Beat 2 can still catch Ulfe unaware.

**The loud way:** If a sentry reaches the **warhorn on the wall** (a full move + action from the fire) and blows it, or if the fight drags past two rounds, **advance the clock to Stirring.** The horn echoes down the mountain.

### The bridge (environmental hazard)

The rope bridge is the star of this fight. It's **difficult terrain** (planks, sway) and holds one Medium creature safely; a second makes it lurch.
- A creature can **cut a bridge rope** (adjacent, action, or a ranged hit vs. Armor 12): the bridge drops to a single-rope swing. Anyone on it when it drops rolls **Agility, Hard (15)** or falls — `3d6` and into the cold stream forty feet down (climb out: Athletics, Hard).
- The orcs know the bridge; a smart sentry will **wait for a PC to be mid-span and cut it.** Telegraph the frayed ropes so the party can play the same trick.

### Encounter — Threat ~10

- **3 × Orc Raider** (Threat 2 each) — the sentries; brave, straightforward, will make for the horn if losing.
- **1 × Orc Archer** (Threat 2) — up on a ledge above the gate (half cover), if the party is loud.
- The **bridge & chasm** — no threat cost, but it *is* the fight. Falls, cover, chokepoint.

Easy opener by the numbers; the terrain makes it interesting. Teaches: chokepoints, cover, and that **the horn is the real objective.** Stat blocks in the bestiary.

### What's here

- On the far side, in the guard-nook: **48 gold** in raid-loot, a **Healing Potion** (`1d8 + 2`), and a child's carved horse from a plundered mule-train — a quiet, ugly reminder of what these raiders are. (House note: humanize the stakes, not the mooks.)
- The defaced dwarven carvings and a scratched orc-tally on the wall tell any dwarf or Lore check (Easy) that *orcs have held this gate a long time, and something drove the last owners out — there are scorch-marks and acid-pitting on the deeper stone.* (First whisper of the dragon.)

## Scene 2 — Great Ulfe's Hall (Combat 2)

Past the bridge, the dwarven entry-hall opens up — pillars, a firepit, filthy bedrolls, and the warband's chief holding court in the seat of the clan he'll never know he's mocking.

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The hall stinks of woodsmoke and wet fur. At its head, on a broken stone throne meant for a dwarf-lord, sprawls the biggest orc you've ever seen — near ogre-sized, one tusk capped in gold, a great **black-bladed axe** across his knees that drinks the firelight and gives none back. Orcs cringe and squabble around the pit. When he sees you, he grins, hefts the black axe like a toy, and roars something that needs no translation.*

**Great Ulfe** is a half-ogre orc: huge, mean, and vain about his "lucky black axe" — which is a **Durgeddin blade** and worth more than everything else in the mountain (see win-button #1). He has no idea. He won't parley; he'll fight, and his orcs fight while he stands.

### Encounter — Threat ~17 (Hard)

- **Great Ulfe** (elite, Threat 8) — half-ogre chieftain, reach, huge damage, carries the black axe.
- **3 × Orc Raider** (Threat 2 each).
- *(Clock — Stirring adds 1 more Orc Raider and denies the party surprise.)*

This is the delve's first hard fight. Ulfe hits like a cart; the raiders swarm. **The orcs break and rout when Ulfe drops** — don't grind the survivors; a fleeing orc that reaches the deep, though, advances the clock.

### The black axe (win-button #1, first copy)

Ulfe's axe is **Durgeddin's black steel**. Whoever takes it holds the thing Nightscale fears (see her block). Let the party feel it's special the instant they lift it: it's cold, perfectly balanced, and there's a name etched in dwarvish under the haft — *Dunhollow-Avenged* — a name that will mean something once they meet the shade below.

### What's here

- **Ulfe's hoard:** **140 gold**, a **Healing Potion**, a jug of orc grog, and a **crude map scratched in charcoal** showing tunnels down to "the wet caves" (Glitterhame) and, further, a sketched **big X over open water with a fanged spiral** — the orcs' warning-mark for the dragon. They pay her a beast every few days and don't go near the lake. (Clue: something big and feared lives in the water below.)

## Scene 3 — The Glitterhame (Investigation, Social, & avoidable Combat)

Below the orc-hall, dwarven corridors give way to **natural caverns** — wet limestone glittering with mineral veins (hence the name), a wide central cavern, mushroom groves, and the sound of running water everywhere. The **troglodytes** den in the far southwest. In a side-cage of lashed stalagmites, they keep a prisoner.

This is the **breather and the key** — where the party gets the lore, the win-buttons, and a friend. It can be run *without a fight* if the party is careful.

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The passage opens into a cathedral of stone. Your light throws back a thousand glints from wet mineral. Pale mushrooms cluster in glowing drifts. Water threads everywhere, running downhill into the dark. The reptile-stink hits you a moment before you hear them — a hissing clatter, off to the left, where lizard-things move in the gloom. And from a crude cage near the water, a hoarse voice, speaking Common with a Bridghaven burr: "You there — you're not orcs. Gods be good, you're not orcs. Quiet, quiet, don't wake the trogs — get me out and I'll make it worth every scar."*

### Yorrin Ashfell (the prisoner — the party's living key)

*Want:* Out. And, more than he'll admit, to be the dwarf who *found Durgeddin's forge* and lived. He came seeking his people's history and got caught by the trogs a week ago.
*Weird thing:* He's not afraid of the orcs or the trogs. He's afraid of the **water** — he flinches from the running streams and won't look downhill. He's *seen* what's down there.

Freeing Yorrin (bend the cage bars, Might Hard; or pick the lashings, Agility Easy) earns the party the **three-clue backbone** of the whole delve. Spread these across his talk — he's grateful, garrulous, and terrified (full dialogue in [cast-and-dialogue.md](cast-and-dialogue.md)):

- **Durgeddin's story** — *"This was Khundrukar. Durgeddin the Black forged here — the finest orc-killing steel that ever was, black as a moonless mine. The orcs came and butchered his whole clan for it. There's a forge two levels down, and they say his ghost still swings the hammer, mad as the day they died. The black blades? Some are still down there. One of 'em cut the dragon once, when she was small — she's never forgotten it."* **(→ win-button #1: black steel hurts Nightscale.)**
- **The dragon** — *"There's a young black wyrm in the flooded deep — the trogs feed her, the gray dwarves pay her. She fights from the water. Dives, heals, comes up spitting acid, dives again. You'll never pin her while she's got her lake."* **(→ the boss's whole gimmick, so the party can plan the counter.)**
- **The waterworks** — *"Dwarves don't let water do as it likes. There are old sluice-gates — great chained wheels — that hold back the Mere's seep into the deep. The forge-level has the controls. My grandsire's notes said the smiths could* drain *the lower cavern in minutes if the works still turn."* **(→ win-button #2: drain the lake, strand the dragon.)**

If the party never frees or talks to Yorrin, seed the same three facts elsewhere: the dragon-mark on Ulfe's map, dwarven **carvings in the Foundry** showing the sluice-mechanism and a wyrmling fleeing a black blade, and the shade's own lament. **Three independent sources, per the house rule** — never gate the delve on rescuing one NPC. But Yorrin is the richest source, and a good party will want him.

Yorrin won't go *down* (he'll wait in the bolt-hole or make for the surface), but he'll happily point the way and translate dwarvish.

### 3b — The troglodyte cave (avoidable Combat — Threat ~14)

The trogs den in the southwest and hold the direct route down. The party can **skirt them** entirely by taking the wet eastern crawl (Yorrin knows it; or a Wilderness/Perception check spots the draft). Skirting = no fight, no clock. Charging the den = this encounter:

- **4 × Troglodyte** (Threat 2 each) — claws and stench.
- **1 × Troglodyte Mystic** (Threat 4) — leads, calls the others, throws rock-magic.

The **Stench** (see block) is the danger — a Poisoned aura that stacks on the whole front line. Fire and light rout them fast (they're photophobic cave things). **They break and flee when the Mystic falls.**

### What's here (the bolt-hole & healing)

- **Glowcap mushrooms** — a drift of them; **eating one** (action) heals `1d6` and can be done twice per person before they turn sour. Free, seeded healing.
- **The Bolt-hole** — behind a concealed dwarven door (Perception Hard, or Yorrin knows it): a dry, barrable sentry-cell. The **one full rest** of the delve. Remember its cost: sleeping wakes the mountain a full threshold. Yorrin will shelter here.

## The Sinkhole (optional hazard — the roper in the dark)

Between the Glitterhame and the Foundry, the direct stair is blocked by a collapse; the way around runs through a **flooded sinkhole** — a black, dripping cavern with a subterranean river and a lot of stalagmites. One of those stalagmites is a **Roper**.

This is an **optional squeeze**, not a required fight — a lurking predator that makes the dark feel dangerous.

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The river fills this cave nearly wall to wall; you pick along a ledge of wet stone spires. The dark eats your light. One of the spires, you realize far too late, has an eye.*

- **1 × Roper** (Threat 6) — grapples with sticky tendrils from range, reels victims in, bites. A single scary monster; see block.
- **Avoiding it:** a careful party (quiet, watching the ceiling, Perception Hard) spots it before it strikes and can **slip past along the far ledge** or **ford the river** (Athletics, cold and Hard, but bloodless). Reward the avoidance.
- If fought, it's a nasty control-fight in the dark — but it **can't leave its spot** (it's rooted), so a party that disengages and runs *can simply leave.* Don't let it become a TPK; it's a hazard, not a boss.

No clock advance either way — the roper is a loner and doesn't call for help.

## Scene 4 — The Foundry (Combat & set-piece)

The heart of Khundrukar: Durgeddin's great forge. Dwarven halls, cold furnaces the duergar have relit, an anvil the size of a cart, and — everywhere — the ghost of industry turned to grief. This is where **both win-buttons live**, where the **tragic core** waits, and where the delve's second hard fight happens.

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *Heat and hammer-ring reach you before the light does. The Foundry is a vast dwarven workhall: dead furnaces down one wall, two of them coughing fresh red now; racks of half-finished black blades; a great anvil under a chimney of dark. Squat gray-skinned dwarves — colorless eyes, joyless faces — are crating blades and hauling them toward a water-channel where a flat **barge** waits. They haven't seen you. And at the far end, half in shadow, a **broad dwarf-shape in a smith's apron** raises a hammer, brings it down on cold iron with no spark at all, and raises it again. Over and over. It does not seem to notice the gray dwarves at all.*

The **duergar** salvage crew is looting the cache. The broad shape is **Durgeddin's shade**, locked in his last, endless task. The duergar have learned to work around him — he only rouses if the forge or the blades are *threatened by orc-kind*, and to his ghost-eyes, everyone is orc-kind at first.

### The duergar (Combat — Threat ~17, Hard)

- **Xazandra the Overseer** (duergar leader, elite, Threat 5) — commands, enlarges, turns invisible once.
- **3 × Duergar** (Threat 3 each) — gray-dwarf salvagers; enlarge, resilient.
- *(Clock — Roused: the forge doors are sealed, the barge is already **half-loaded and casting off** — see below — and the fight starts with the duergar braced, not surprised.)*

The duergar are **mercenary, not fanatical.** They'll parley: they don't care about the party's quarrel with the orcs or the dragon; they want the steel and a clear exit. A silver-tongued party can **cut a deal** (let them take a share of blades and leave — losing some reward, gaining the fight for free) or **turn them loose on the dragon** (they hate paying her tribute). If it comes to blows, **Xazandra flees to the Black Lake when bloodied**, becoming a boss-fight add (and a foreshadow — she runs *away* from the party and *toward* the water, because she'd rather face the party than be caught between).

### Durgeddin's shade (the tragic core — no threat cost unless provoked)

This is the beating, broken heart of the dungeon. Handle it as roleplay, not combat, unless the party swings first.

- The shade won't attack unless the **blades or forge are threatened** or someone raises a weapon to him. If the party **shows him a Durgeddin blade** (Ulfe's axe, or one from the racks), his hammer *stops.* He turns. He speaks — in old dwarvish (Yorrin, or a Lore check, translates) — and what comes out is two hundred years of grief (full text in [cast-and-dialogue.md](cast-and-dialogue.md)):

> *"You carry Dunhollow-Avenged. Then the muster has come at last. Where is the army, kinsman? I forged for all of you. I forged and forged. Where did everyone... where did they..."*

- He believes the party are his kin, arrived at last for the war he armed. He does not know his clan is two centuries dead — or he cannot bear to. **The party's choice** (house-style moral beat, keep it light and player-driven):
  - **Tell him the truth** — the war is over, his people are at rest, there is no army coming. A brutal kindness. If they land it well (a genuine Charisma moment, or simply honest words), the shade *finally* sets down the hammer. He blesses the blade they carry (it becomes attuned to strike Nightscale — see reward), points them to the sluice-controls himself, and fades. *Peace.*
  - **Take the blades and go** — practical, and he'll let them arm from the racks, still hammering, still waiting. The cache is theirs; the grief stays lit. No penalty, but no peace. (House note: no wrong answer, but the table will *feel* the difference.)
  - **Fight him** — if provoked, he's a dangerous **Forge-Shade** (Threat 7, see block); a needless, sad fight. Avoid steering them here.

### The set-pieces in the Foundry

- **The black-blade cache (win-button #1, the real stock).** Racks and crates of Durgeddin's steel — as many **Durgeddin blades** as the party has hands for (see reward). *These are the answer to the dragon.* If the clock hit **Roused**, the barge is loading: every round of delay/dithering, narrate another crate poled off downriver — fewer blades to take. If the party lets the barge escape entirely, they salvage only **one** black blade from the wreckage of the fight. Pressure without a hard gate.
- **The sluice-controls (win-button #2).** A dwarven mechanism of chained wheels and a great lever-bank against the downhill wall, with a **carved diagram** above it: the mountain in cross-section, the Dark Mere up top, the Black Lake below, and arrows showing the water *draining out* when the wheels turn. Reading it (any Lore/Investigation, Easy — or Yorrin/the shade tells them) reveals: **turning the wheels here drains the Black Lake.** They can't fully work it from here (the drains are *at* the lake), but now they know the plan — and they can **spend an action at the lake's own gate-wheel** to trigger it during the boss fight. (If you prefer, let a PC stay in the Foundry and work it remotely across two rounds — reward creative splitting.)
- **A working dwarven trap** (the delve's "perfectly working trap"): the approach to the cache is warded by a **blade-trap in the floor-plates** (Perception Hard to spot the seams; Agility Hard or Disable to cross safe; `3d6` slashing and knocked Prone if triggered — and triggering it **advances the clock**, it's loud). Telegraph the worn dwarvish glyph — a warning, if they can read it.
- **A dwarven mercy-cache:** in a shrine-nook to Moradin, **2 × Healing Potion** and a flask of **fire-oil** (throw, action, `2d6` fire in a square — useful and thematic against the acid-dragon). Seeded healing before the boss.

**Before they descend to the lake, make sure the party has at least one Durgeddin blade and knows about the drain.** Both were offered three ways. If they somehow have neither, that's your cue to run Nightscale on her softest dials (see her block) — not to punish blind players.

## Scene 5 — The Black Lake (Boss)

The tunnel from the Foundry slopes down, the air turns wet and sour, and the walls show old acid-pitting. It opens onto a vast flooded cavern: **the Black Lake**, still and black and cold, fed by water weeping down from the Dark Mere far above. This is Nightscale's hall.

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The cavern is enormous and half-drowned — a black mirror of water under a ceiling lost in dark, a shore of wet gravel and old bones, and glints across the water where a hoard lies half-sunk. The acid-stink is everywhere. On a shelf of rock above the waterline, coiled around her plunder, a dragon the color of wet coal lifts her head — young, sleek, all cruelty and appetite. Water sheets off her scales. She looks at the black blade in your hand, and for just a heartbeat, something older than her hunger crosses her eyes. Then she smiles, all teeth. "You brought me a* toy*," she purrs. "How thoughtful. I'll have it back the way I got the first one — out of a dead dwarf's hand."*

**Nightscale** is a young black dragon: acid breath, amphibious, vicious, and vain. She is beatable — *if* the party uses what the mountain gave them. Play her cocky and cruel, with one crack in the armor: she is genuinely afraid of the black steel, and she covers it with mockery.

### Encounter — Threat ~18 (solo boss)

- **Nightscale**, young black dragon (boss, Threat 18). Full block below.
- *(Clock — Roused: **Xazandra the Overseer** (Threat 5) is here, having fled the Foundry; she'll fight beside the dragon to save her own skin, or bargain to switch sides if the party is winning.)*
- *(Clock — Awake: Nightscale starts submerged and alert, and has her **Frightful Roar** available round one. If she was never roused, the party can catch her basking — a **surprise round** if they approached quietly.)*

### The two win-buttons in play

This is where the seeds pay off:

1. **Black steel bites.** Any PC striking with a **Durgeddin blade** ignores Nightscale's damage resistance and adds **+1d6**. The first time a black blade *hits* her, she recoils — she loses her next Submerge and can be pinned that round (narrate the old wound, the flash of fear). Brandishing one gives a PC **advantage** on rolls to resist her Frightful Roar. *This is why they came up the mountain with a dead orc's axe.*
2. **Drain the lake.** A PC at the **lake gate-wheel** (on the shore, clearly dwarven, clearly the thing from the Foundry diagram) can spend an action on a **Might or Mechanism check, Hard (15)** to start the drain. Once started, the lake **empties over three rounds.** As it drains:
   - Round 1–2: the water drops; Nightscale's Submerge still works but the "pool" shrinks.
   - End of round 3: the lake is **mudflats.** Nightscale can no longer Submerge or dive-heal, loses her acid-water footing, and fights at **disadvantage** — a black dragon flopping in the mud is a *land* fight, and she knows it. She may panic and try to flee up the drain-tunnels (let a cornered, drained dragon beg or bolt — a memorable end either way).

A party that lands **both** — black blades *and* the drain — will feel like legends. A party that lands neither is in a very hard solo-dragon fight; soften her (dials below).

### Nightscale — boss stat block

> **Nightscale** *(young black dragon)* — Tier 2 · boss · Threat 18
> **HP** 120 · **Armor** 17 · **Mind** 14 · **Spirit** 13 · **Charisma** 15 · **Speed** 8, swim 12, fly 10
> *Attack:* **Bite** `+7 vs. Armor → 2d8 + 4` acid.
> *Attack:* **Claws** `+7 vs. Armor → 2d6 + 4` — as her action she may claw twice (two rolls) instead of biting.
> *Attack:* **Acid Breath** (recharge: roll a d6 at the start of her turn, recharges on 5–6) — a line 8 squares long: everything in it takes `6d6` acid, **Agility vs. `+7` for half.** Her signature; she leads with it.
> *Special — Amphibious / Submerge.* She breathes water and air. As her move, she can **dive into the Black Lake**: while submerged she has **full cover** (can't be targeted) and **regains 10 HP** at the start of her next turn, then surfaces anywhere on the lake to attack. *This is negated entirely once the lake is drained (see win-button #2), and skipped the round after a black blade first strikes her (win-button #1).*
> *Special — Frightful Roar* (once, or round one if **Awake**). Each enemy within Near rolls **Charisma vs. `+7`** or is **Frightened** of her until the end of its next turn. A PC brandishing a **Durgeddin blade** has **advantage** on this roll.
> *Special — Dragon's Vigor.* On her turn she acts with a swiftness lesser things don't: once per round, after any creature's turn, she may **move up to her swim speed** (staying in initiative order otherwise). Keeps her mobile and slippery on the water. *(Lost while stranded on the mudflats.)*
> *Vulnerability — Black Steel.* A **Durgeddin blade** ignores her hide (no resistance) and deals **+1d6**; the first hit costs her a Submerge. She *fears* these blades.
> *Resistances:* Resists non-magical, non-black-steel weapon damage (thick hide) — halve it. **Immune to acid.**
> *Notes — running her:* Open with Acid Breath from the water, Submerge, surface elsewhere, breathe again on recharge. Bite the black-blade wielder to try to disarm-by-death. She is **vain and cruel but not suicidal** — stranded on drained mudflats and facing black steel, a young dragon *breaks*: she'll beg, bargain (offer the hoard, offer to leave the mountain forever), or bolt up a drain-tunnel. Let the players decide if a fleeing wyrm gets to live. Killing her ends the delve; sparing her plants a splendid future grudge.
> *(**Softening dials** — if the party found neither win-button, or is getting wiped: HP 95; Acid Breath `5d6`; she Submerges only every *other* round (greedy to keep fighting); drop Xazandra. The fight should be desperate, not hopeless.)*

### The hoard

Sunk in the shallows and heaped on her shelf: **Nightscale's plunder** — the reward math is in Resolution. Grabbing it mid-fight is possible but risky (she guards it); an amphibious grab in a draining lake is a great scramble beat.

## Resolution

If the party brings down Nightscale (or drives her out and takes her lake):

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The last echo of the dragon dies, and Khundrukar is only a mountain again — cold, and old, and finally quiet. Water drips. Somewhere far above, if you listen, you could almost hear a hammer stop. On the shelf, under two centuries of stolen coin and a wyrm's greedy hoarding, the black blades glint — the last work of a dwarf who forged a war no one ever came to fight. You came. Two hundred years too late, and exactly in time.*

**How clean the win is** depends on the clock and the win-buttons:
- **Ran quiet, both win-buttons, laid the shade to rest:** a legend. They stranded a dragon, honored the dead, and walk out arm-laden. Kess's whole caravan-town will know their names.
- **Woke the mountain, lost the barge, brute-forced the dragon:** they win bloody — fewer blades, a harder boss, maybe a PC down. Still a hell of a story.
- **Failed the delve / fled:** the raids go on, Kess pays only the advance, and Nightscale now *knows there are dwarves and black blades about* — she'll come looking. A grim hook.

### Rewards

- **500 gold** total from Kess (200 advance + 300 on return), **plus Nightscale's hoard:** roughly **600–900 gold** in coin and sunk plate, a **pair of uncommon gems** (~100g each), and **2 × Healing Potion** the duergar had stashed for the tribute-run. Generous — this is the climax of a hard delve, near the top of the Apprentice-tier adventure band ([../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md](../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md)).
- Each PC gains **1 level** (milestone leveling to **level 5**) — at level 5 each takes an **Ability +1** ([../../rules/03-progression.md](../../rules/03-progression.md)). Still Apprentice tier (+2 training) until level 8.
- **The signature magic item — a Durgeddin Blade** (one per PC who wants one; they're a *cache*, and that's the point):
  > **Durgeddin's Black Steel** — *uncommon.* A weapon of Durgeddin's make (choose the form to fit the wielder — axe, blade, hammer). It **never chips or dulls**, and against **orcs, goblinoids, giants, and dragonkind** it deals **+1d4 damage** and ignores their damage resistance. If the party **laid the shade to rest**, one chosen blade is *Dunhollow-Avenged* itself: additionally **+1d6 vs. dragons** and sheds a faint cold light in the presence of orc-kind within Near — Durgeddin's last blessing, finally spent on something worth killing.
- **Yorrin Ashfell** lives, gets his life's find, and becomes a **contact in Bridghaven** — a dwarf loremaster who owes the party everything and knows where a dozen other ruins sleep. (Campaign hook engine.)
- **Information / hooks:**
  - The duergar came *up* from somewhere deeper. Where? And they were **buying** black blades to arm someone — who wants two hundred orc-killing swords, and why?
  - If Nightscale fled, she's out there, grown and vengeful, and she remembers their faces.
  - Durgeddin's forge still stands. In the right hands, it could arm a war — the way it was always meant to. Whose war?

### Failure modes

- **TPK at the dragon:** Nightscale doesn't eat corpses that carry black steel — she *takes the blades* and hoards them, and leaves the bodies for the trogs. The next party Kess hires finds the fallen PCs' gear in a wyrm's hoard: a recovery/vengeance hook, and a chance to loot back your own legend.
- **Fled the mountain:** they live, poorer and shaken; the north road stays closed; and somewhere below, a dragon and a grieving ghost keep their long, patient watch. Come back stronger.
