# 20 — Ancestries (the catalog)

> The full roster of playable ancestries. Character creation ([02-character-creation.md](02-character-creation.md)) keeps a six-race starter set and points here for the rest — the same way it points to [19-classes.md](19-classes.md) for the class roster.

## How an ancestry works

Pick **one ancestry**. It gives you exactly two things, at level 1:

- **`+1` to one ability** (fixed per ancestry — a nudge toward a role, never a lock). The lone exception is the **Human**, whose `+1` goes anywhere.
- **One Racial Signature** — a single trait. It is **one of your two level-1 signatures** (the other is your Class Signature, [19-classes.md](19-classes.md)). You get exactly **one**, always.

A "trait" here is a small **bundle**, not a single clause — look at the classics: the Dwarf's is *"darkvision **and** advantage vs. fear/charm,"* the Elf's is *"dim-light sight **and** advantage on Perception."* Every trait below is tuned to that same power level. No ancestry stacks two traits; the catalog is large because **families have many members**, not because anyone gets more.

> **Families are flavor, not a mechanical layer.** "Elf" or "Dragonborn" is a grouping for browsing. You choose **one member** (e.g., *Wood Elf*) and get its one ability and one trait. There is no "base trait + subrace trait."

## The trait toolbox (for the GM building new ancestries)

Every trait is built from effects already in the game. The legal palette:

- **Advantage** on a named skill, or on a defense vs. a condition (fear, charm, Poisoned…).
- A **sense** — dim-light sight, or darkvision (see in the dark ~30 ft).
- **1/rest:** reroll a d20; or drop to 1 HP instead of 0.
- **Movement:** Speed 8; a swim or climb speed; breathe water; move through larger creatures' spaces; ignore a kind of difficult terrain; glide (no fall damage).
- **Resistance** to one damage type (take half).
- **Pack Tactics** — advantage on attacks while an ally is adjacent to your target.
- **Innate magic** — know one **cantrip** (cast with your primary, 0 mana), or a specific **1/rest** Lesser spell (e.g., *Misty Step*), no mana.
- **Breath weapon** — 1/rest, a Close 2-square cone; roll `d20 + primary` vs. each creature's Armor for `2d6` of an element.
- **Natural weapon** — claws / horns / bite, a `1d6` weapon you always carry (uses your primary).
- **Flat `+1` to a static defense** (e.g., natural `+1` Armor — bounded, like Bulwark).
- A **narrow immunity** (can't be magically slept; immune to disease) or **cosmetic shapeshift**.

> **The fundamentals stay off-limits.** No racial bonus to **attack rolls**, to the **training bonus**, or to **mana-pool size**; no **extra action**; no **stacking `+1`s on a d20**. Flat bonuses to *static defenses* are fine. If a homebrew trait would touch one of those, it's out.

## What ancestry doesn't decide

- **Your class.** The `+1` nudges, it doesn't gate — a Might-favoring Orc can be a caster; a Charisma Tiefling can swing a greataxe.
- **Your pool, perks, or signatures-beyond-the-racial.** Those come from class and level ([19-classes.md](19-classes.md), [03-progression.md](03-progression.md)).
- **Language, age, height as stats.** Flavor only ([02-character-creation.md](02-character-creation.md)).

If a player wants an ancestry not listed, the GM builds one from the toolbox above: **`+1` to one ability + one trait of comparable power.** That's the whole recipe.
