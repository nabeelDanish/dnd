# The Vermilion Garden — Cast, Secrets & Dialogue

The run-at-the-table NPC reference for [adventure.md](adventure.md). One entry per character: how to play them, **what they're hiding**, **what's free vs. what's behind a check** (and which check), and **dialogue samples** for the situations they'll actually come up in. Keep the plot logic (the clock, the evidence web, the scenes) in the main file; keep *this* open while you talk.

## How to read an entry

Each entry has the same parts:

- **Read / Voice / Wants** — the one-line handle, how they sound, what drives them.
- **Hiding** — their secret (almost everyone has one; only Florian's is murder).
- **Free (no roll)** — what they'll tell *anyone* who asks, and what the party simply observes. Per [../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md](../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md), **don't gate this behind dice** — if a player asks, give it.
- **Gated** — what takes a check, written as *what you want* → **ability (training) target** → result. Social rolls are **Charisma** (Persuasion / Intimidation / Deception / Performance); reading whether they're *lying* is **Mind (Insight)**; bribery is **gold + Charisma**. A genuinely good roleplay approach can **waive the roll** — reward it. Use the tiered bands from [../../rules/16-co-gm-guide.md](../../rules/16-co-gm-guide.md) (`<10` nothing/cost · `10–14` the gist · `15–19` the answer · `20+` answer + a bonus thread).
- **Won't give** — hard limits; what no roll pries loose (so you don't accidentally hand over the ending).
- **Dialogue** — sample lines by situation. Paraphrase freely; these set the voice.
- **Points to** — where they hand the trail next.

> **The golden rule for this cast:** the killer is found by **evidence**, never by vibe. Almost everyone is warm and hiding *something*; almost none of it is the murder. A great Persuasion makes an NPC tell the truth *they know* — it never makes an innocent guilty or turns Florian's gentleness into a confession before the party has the goods. See [adventure.md](adventure.md) → *Running a mystery*.

# THE KILLER

## Florian Sael — perfumer & garden-keeper *(Half-Elf)*

**Read:** the most-loved man in the Verdance, and the murderer. **Voice:** warm, soft, funny, self-deprecating; presses gifts on you; asks after your mother. Never rattled. **Wants:** to make his dead daughter *bloom* before Midsummer — and to stay the trusted friend he genuinely is to the district.

**Hiding:** he is the killer. He drains the district's most radiant people to feed the **Daughter-Rose** — a girl-shaped briar grown over the remains of his daughter **Mira** (dead of summer-fever on Midsummer, eight years gone). He believes, sincerely, that he is being *kind.*

**Free (no roll):** grief for the victims (real); generous help with the investigation; gossip that steers gently toward Dama Vesh or "a madman in for the festival"; a free vial of his festival attar. That his roses are the finest in the south (proud, public). He **will not** open the conservatory ("the grafts are delicate").

**Gated:**
- *Notice the over-explaining* — when shown a vermilion rose he admires it, then talks too long about who he gives them to → **Mind (Insight) 15** → reads as a tell: *he's covering.* (Doesn't prove anything yet.)
- *Get into the conservatory by charm* — **Charisma (Persuasion/Deception) 20**, and only with real leverage ("opening the door is the one way to clear your name") → he relents, improvising a lie the whole way down. The Daughter-Rose is right there → straight to the climax.
- *Crack him* — there is **no roll** that makes Florian confess cold. He breaks only when **confronted with the effigy**, or boxed in with **rose + pattern + Mira** together (see climax, below).

**Won't give:** the crime, the conservatory, or any hint of Mira — until cornered with hard evidence. Charm alone never works; he is *certain he is innocent of any wrong.*

**Dialogue —**
- *First meeting (at the garden):* "She sang in my shop while I worked, you know. Lysenne. Gods, the quiet now. — Here, take this, it's nothing, a little midsummer. You'll find who did this? Please. To *them*, of all people."
- *Asked about the victims:* "Castan I crowned with my own hands. Pateros gave my apprentice sweets when he was small. They were the warm ones. The bright ones. Who hates *warmth*?" *(GM: note the selection criteria hiding in plain sight.)*
- *Mira named (gentle):* *(a long stillness)* "…You've been talking to old Dama, then. Yes. I had a daughter. It was a long time ago. Forgive me — I don't." *(changes the subject.)*
- *Mira pressed (hard):* *(the warmth thins, just slightly)* "Be careful what you dig up in a man's garden, friend. Some things are planted deep for a reason."
- *Shown his own rose:* "Oh — that's *mine.* Sael's Vermilion. No one else grows it — well. I give them away by the fistful at the festival, so… anyone might have one. Anyone at all." *(too eager; Insight 15.)*
- *At the effigy (climax):* "You weren't supposed to see her yet. She's not finished. One more warmth and she'd open her eyes — Please. Don't make me hurt you. It's almost kind."

**Points to:** everywhere but himself — Dama Vesh, "some stranger," the Briar Bride. The truth he can't help leaking: the rose, and the silence around Mira.

# THE GENTLE ONES — *Florian's camouflage (all innocent)*

*Run these with the exact warmth and grief you give Florian, so "kind soul with a dead loved one and a locked room" can't finger him.*

## Edran — the gardens' beekeeper *(Firbolg)*

**Read:** a gentle giant who gives honeycomb to every child; the *honest* version of Florian's cover. **Voice:** slow, kind, a little sad; long pauses. **Wants:** to be left to his bees and his boy's small grave.

**Hiding:** nothing criminal. He lost a son to the same fever and grieves quietly.

**Free (no roll):** he's in the gardens every dawn and dusk (bees keep those hours); he carries a thin curved hive-knife; he lost a child years ago. He saw nothing useful — or did he? (see gated).

**Gated:**
- *Anything he half-saw on a murder dawn* — **Charisma (Persuasion) 10** (he's willing, just vague) → a figure with a basket in the high terraces before light, "but that's only the flower-folk, gathering — Florian, or one of his." *(Corroborates opportunity without him suspecting a soul.)*

**Won't give:** suspicion of Florian — it would never cross his mind. He'll defend him.

**Dialogue —**
- *Accused:* *(wounded, not angry)* "You think *I*— with these hands? I keep bees, friend. I've buried what I love. I don't make more graves."
- *Asked about dawns:* "There's always someone among the roses before the sun. Me and my hives. Florian and his baskets. The garden doesn't sleep, so neither do we."

**Points to:** unwittingly, the dawn flower-cart (→ Florian's opportunity).

## Lensa — the memorial dollmaker *(Forest Gnome)*

**Read:** the sharpest misdirection in the game — a tender artist who *recreates the dead*, with a locked room full of the victims' faces. **Voice:** soft, precise, motherly. **Wants:** to do right by the grieving families.

**Hiding:** nothing but the ordinary privacy of grief-work.

**Free (no roll):** she makes memorial dolls (an old Verdance custom); families commissioned dolls of **all three victims**; she asked those families intimate questions about the dead to make each doll *true*; she keeps the work in a **locked studio** until finished.

**Gated:**
- *Get into the locked studio (suspecting horror)* — **Charisma (Persuasion) 15** or a B&E (Sleight/Stealth 15) → a shrine of grief: three half-finished dolls of Lysenne, Pateros, Castan, and notebooks of tender details. **Damning at a glance, innocent on inspection.** Burns a night and clears nothing.

**Won't give:** any wrongdoing — there is none. If the party is cruel about it, she's quietly devastated (and the Witherrows/temple folk hear of it).

**Dialogue —**
- *First meeting:* "You want to see the dolls? They're not ready. The families come when they're ready, not before. Grief has a season, like roses."
- *Studio revealed:* "Yes — all three. Their mothers asked. I had to know how Lysenne laughed, what Pateros loved, to make them true. Is that… is that what you thought I was?"

**Points to:** nowhere — a dead end that *teaches* the table: kindness + dead faces + a locked room ≠ guilt. (Mirror it back at Florian later.)

## Marise & the fever-bereaved *(mixed ancestries)*

**Read:** the chorus that drowns Florian's motive. **Voice:** quiet communal grief. **Wants:** for the lost children to be remembered.

**Free (no roll):** eight years ago the **summer-fever killed a cohort of the district's children on one Midsummer**; *many* beloved locals lost someone; the temple holds a remembrance the eve of Midsummer; Marise tends the public memorial rose-bed — "Florian planted it for us, bless him." So *"lost a child, grieves on Midsummer"* fits a dozen gentle people.

**Won't give:** any single suspect — that's the point.

**Dialogue —**
- *Asked about old griefs:* "Half this quarter lit a candle that year. My Tomas. The cooper's twins. Florian's little Mira. We don't speak the names much — it's kinder."

**Points to:** Mira's name (a thread), buried in a crowd of equally grieving parents.

# THE LOUD DECOYS — *the cynical-instinct traps (all innocent of murder)*

## Castellan Vane — theater impresario *(Drow)* — **the decoy villain**

**Read:** the suspect the table will *want* — theatrical, death-obsessed, secretive. **Voice:** sonorous, delighted, quoting his own play. **Wants:** to be the most fascinating person in any room; to keep his side-trade quiet.

**Hiding:** he **fences grave-robbed funerary art**, and **bought some of Mira Sael's grave-goods** years ago from Skein. So the trail *"who disturbed the dead?"* runs through his gallery. **Not the killer.**

**Free (no roll):** he adores the murders as "operatic"; he wrote *The Briar Bride's Kiss*; the victims orbited his theater; he keeps a locked private gallery. He'll *flirt* with suspicion deliberately.

**Gated:**
- *See the gallery* — **Charisma (Persuasion/Deception) 15**, a bribe to stagehands (**~25g + Charisma 10**), or B&E (**15**) → funerary masks, mourning-jewelry, relics with the temple seal — **screams killer, is a fence's stash.**
- *Steal/read his ledger* — **Sleight of Hand 15** or persuasion of a clerk → records his buys, **including a child's grave-goods bought from "Skein."** → the linchpin thread.
- *Catch the truth he's hiding* — **Charisma (Intimidation) 15** or **Insight 15** on his deflections → he confesses to *fencing*, indignant: "Murder? I am an *artist*, not an animal."

**Won't give:** a murder confession (he didn't), nor Skein's hideout willingly (Intimidation 15 or the ledger gets the name).

**Dialogue —**
- *First meeting:* "Three of the loveliest creatures in the Verdance, arranged among flowers like a final act. Whoever it is has *taste*, I'll grant them that. …Oh, don't look at me like that. Though I confess I enjoy that you did."
- *Accused outright:* "Me? *Marvelous.* No — I traffic in the *dead's leavings*, darling, not the dead. Rings, lockets, a saint's finger if the price is right. Grave-robbing's cousin, not murder's."
- *Pressed about a child's grave-goods:* "…A small lot, years back. Trinkets off a little one. Skein brought them — he's the one with the spade, in the Witherrows. I only *buy.*"

**Points to:** **Skein** (and thus, eventually, "the flower-man"). When the party busts him and feels they've won — that's the cue for the Night-2 body to prove them wrong.

## Madame Ysolde — festival patroness *(High Elf)*

**Read:** beloved hostess with a real motive — for *one* victim. **Voice:** gracious, witty, a half-beat too smooth. **Wants:** to keep her ruin and her side-trade hidden; to keep being adored.

**Hiding:** she's in debt and selling a forbidden euphoric **"bliss-attar."** **Lysenne found out and threatened to expose her** — Ysolde was trying to *buy her silence*, not kill her. Innocent of murder; guilty of dealing and lying.

**Free (no roll):** she funds the festival and theater; she and Lysenne "had a falling-out" (she's vague); she has no connection to Pateros or Castan.

**Gated:**
- *Her whereabouts the night Lysenne died* — **Insight 15** catches the evasion; **Persuasion 15 / Intimidation 15** → she admits she met Lysenne to *pay her off*, not harm her.
- *The bliss-attar* — **Investigation 15** of her accounts, a stolen ledger (**Sleight 15**), or **Intimidation 15** → the drug trade and the blackmail motive spill out.

**Won't give:** the other two murders — she has no link and will rightly point that out, dissolving herself as a suspect.

**Dialogue —**
- *About Lysenne:* "We quarreled. Women do. It was nothing." *(Insight 15: it was very much something.)*
- *Cornered:* "Fine. *Fine.* She knew about the attar. She was going to ruin me. I went to *pay her*, do you understand? I needed her quiet, not dead — a corpse can't keep a secret, it *becomes* one."

**Points to:** a hot, narrow dead end (Victim 1 only). Testing her against Pateros and Castan clears her.

## Deputy Roon — Watch officer *(Hobgoblin)*

**Read:** the "helpful" cop whose help misleads. **Voice:** breezy, obliging, always a favor to offer. **Wants:** his racket undiscovered.

**Hiding:** a **protection racket** on festival vendors, and he **buried a witness statement** — flower-girl **Nessa** saw the dawn flower-cart — because chasing it would expose where *he* was that night (shaking down a stall).

**Free (no roll, or for a bribe):** the case file, morgue access, a blind eye to move at night (**bribe ~20g + Charisma 10**). His file is *curated* — it omits Nessa's statement and leans toward Dama Vesh.

**Gated:**
- *The buried statement* — **Intimidation 15**, catching his racket (**Investigation 15**), B&E the evidence locker (**Stealth/Sleight 15**), or reaching **Nessa** directly → the **flower-cart on the high terrace before dawn** (→ Florian's opportunity).
- *His racket* — **Insight 15** on his too-smooth helpfulness, or vendor testimony → leverage to flip him.

**Won't give:** the buried clue *for free* — his own neck depends on it staying buried. Make the party pry.

**Dialogue —**
- *Helpful front:* "Anything you need, friends — file's here, the bodies are at the temple, and between us? The Witherrows woman's worth a look. Nasty piece. Grows the red roses, found over a corpse. I'd start there."
- *Cornered on the racket:* "All right — a few coins from the stalls to keep 'em *safe*, that's all, everybody does it — and yeah, there was a girl, saw a cart. I sat on it. Couldn't have her talking about that dawn, could I? But I never touched anybody, I swear it on the Watch."

**Points to:** **Nessa** and the flower-cart — once the party makes him cough it up.

# THE SCAPEGOATS — *the answer the district wants (both innocent)*

## Dama Vesh — poison-gardener *(Abyssal Tiefling)*

**Read:** the feared "witch" the mob wants — and the single richest source if you earn her. **Voice:** sharp, proud, contemptuous of fools; thaws only for genuine respect. **Wants:** to be left alone; to not be blamed again; to protect the Witherrows folk.

**Hiding:** nothing — but she won't explain herself to people who've already decided she's guilty, which makes her *look* guilty.

**Free (only once she trusts you):** she's no killer; she found Pateros's body and tried to close his eyes; the blood on her shears is from grafting and doctoring.

**Gated (the gate is *respect*, not a number — but if you roll):**
- *The rose distinction* — **Charisma (Persuasion) 15 with a humble approach** (advantage if respectful; **disadvantage or refusal** if threatened) → *"Mine's common red. That on the bodies is Sael's Vermilion — his graft, no one else's for two hundred miles."* (→ Conclusion 1)
- *The wound* — same trust → *"That's a grafting-knife's edge — fine, curved. Not a footpad's blade, not a beast."* (→ Conclusion 2)
- *Mira* — same trust → *"Florian's girl drowned in fever on a Midsummer. He never grieved right. Just kept growing roses where she's planted."* (→ Conclusion 4 / motive)

**Won't give:** anything to anyone who treats her as the witch. **Intimidation backfires** — she stonewalls *and* the district hears the law suspects her (advances the mob clock).

**Dialogue —**
- *Approached as a suspect:* "Come to measure me for the rope, have you? Get off my step."
- *Approached with respect:* *(a long look)* "…Huh. Manners. From the law. All right. Sit. Mind the nightshade. Now — you want to know about that rose, don't you. You should."
- *Defended from the mob:* *(stiff)* "I'd not have wept for any of you. But I'll not forget this either. …The flower-man. Look at the flower-man. No one ever does."

**Points to:** **Florian** — the rose, the wound, and Mira — but only to a party that treats her like a person.

## Grenn — gardens' groundskeeper *(Half-Orc)*

**Read:** the big, surly, bloody-handed one who "obviously did it" — and didn't. **Voice:** clipped, defensive, sweaty under questions. **Wants:** to not get caught for the small thing he *did* do.

**Hiding:** he **robbed Castan's corpse** — lifted a purse and a ring before raising the alarm. Hence the shiftiness and the lies about his movements.

**Free (no roll):** he finds the bodies (dawn rounds); he prunes the beds (the blood/dirt); he disliked Castan ("prancing peacock").

**Gated:**
- *Crack the shiftiness* — **Charisma (Intimidation) 15**, or **Persuasion 15** ("we're after a murderer, not a purse-snatch") → he confesses to **theft, not murder**, and — relieved — gives up that at the dawn he found Castan he saw **Florian's flower-cart up among the high terraces, attar on the breeze, when no one else was about.** (→ Conclusion 4)

**Won't give:** a murder confession (he's innocent). If arrested for murder, the real killer strikes again that night (Clock A).

**Dialogue —**
- *Questioned:* "I find 'em, that's all. Somebody's got to. Don't make me the monster 'cause I'm the one holding the shovel."
- *Cracked:* "All right! I took his purse, and a ring — he didn't *need* it no more! That's all, I swear, just the purse. …Look, you want something useful? That dawn — the flower-man's cart was already up top. Smelled his perfume on the wind. Always up before the birds, that one. Never thought twice."

**Points to:** the **dawn flower-cart** (→ Florian's opportunity).

# THE WIDER WEB — *threads, contacts, and connective tissue (all innocent of murder)*

## Doral — Florian's apprentice perfumer *(Tabaxi)*

**Read:** the inside near-witness, gagged by his own small crime. **Voice:** eager, fond of his master, fidgety when the shop's takings come up. **Wants:** to not lose his place; to not be caught skimming.

**Hiding:** he's been **blending cheap knockoffs of Florian's attar and pocketing the coin.** He has a shop key (not the conservatory) and has *noticed* Florian's pre-dawn absences and the always-locked cellar — but says nothing.

**Free (no roll):** Florian is the kindest master in the city; the conservatory is "his private grafts, no one goes down."

**Gated:**
- *His near-witness knowledge* — **Charisma (Persuasion) 15** (earn his trust) or **Intimidation 15** / catching the skim (**Insight 15**) → Florian is **out alone most nights before dawn**, the **cellar is always locked**, and *"he's been strange since the festival started — humming, happy, like he's waiting for something."* (→ Conclusion 4 + a way into the conservatory)

**Won't give:** he can't *prove* anything and won't believe Florian's a killer; he'll defend him even as he hands you the timeline.

**Dialogue —**
- *Earned:* "He's the best man I know. But… you didn't hear it from me — he's gone every night before light. Gathering, he says. And the cellar — I've worked here three years and I've never once been down. 'The grafts,' he says. Every time."
- *Caught skimming:* "Don't tell him — *please* don't tell him — I'll say whatever you want, only— it's just a little off the top, the tourists can't tell the difference—"

**Points to:** Florian's nights and the locked conservatory.

## Madame Carow — the "Briar Bride" seer *(Changeling)*

**Read:** a charismatic fraud profiting on the panic; the rumor-mill's engine. **Voice:** theatrical, ominous, switches to brisk salesmanship when business is slow. **Wants:** to keep the fear (and her charm-sales) high.

**Hiding:** she **invents and embellishes Briar Bride "sightings"** to keep the terror profitable. Like Florian, she *benefits from the legend* — which makes her briefly suspectable.

**Free (no roll):** she'll sell the full Briar Bride legend, lurid; she'll claim *"three people have seen the Bride this week"* (she made up two of them).

**Gated:**
- *Sort the real sighting from her inventions* — **Insight (Mind) 15** → most "sightings" are her fabrications; one persistent report (the dawn cart) is *not* hers and predates her tale. → don't let her noise bury Nessa's real clue.
- *Catch the fraud* — **Insight 15 / Intimidation 15** → she admits she's "giving the people what they need" and dines out on the dread.

**Won't give:** any true lead — she has none; her value is *negative* (she's why the witness pool is polluted). Useful to unmask so the party stops chasing ghosts.

**Dialogue —**
- *Reading the party:* "The Briar Bride walks again, my dears — she takes the beautiful and leaves a rose. Crossed her path, has someone? A charm for your throats, only two silver, very reasonable—"
- *Fraud exposed:* "And what's the harm? They're frightened anyway. I just… give it a shape. A face sells better than a mystery, loves."

**Points to:** nowhere real — but unmasking her *clears the fog* so genuine witnesses (Nessa, Grenn) stand out.

## Ser Aldric Venn — spurned suitor *(Flame Dragonborn)*

**Read:** the textbook jealous-lover suspect. **Voice:** proud, hot, defensive; a temper on a short fuse. **Wants:** to bury his humiliation.

**Hiding:** he was infatuated with **Lysenne**, was publicly refused, **threatened her** before witnesses, then paid to suppress the gossip — and has no alibi for that night. **Innocent of murder.**

**Free (no roll):** he and Lysenne "were close, once"; he's prickly and evasive about it.

**Gated:**
- *The threat & missing alibi* — **Insight 15** / **Intimidation 15** → the public threat and the paid-off gossip surface; he was drunk and alone that night.
- *Clear or damn him* — testing against Pateros/Castan (no motive, no link) → dissolves him; he's a wounded fool, not a killer.

**Won't give:** murders 2 and 3 — no connection, and he'll furiously say so.

**Dialogue —**
- *Pressed:* "I loved her and she laughed at me in front of the whole quarter. Yes, I said things. Drunk, ugly things. I did not *cut her throat.* Ask me about the old man or the festival boy — I never spoke ten words to either."

**Points to:** a dead end overlapping Ysolde's — both orbit Lysenne, neither is the killer.

## Sexton Crale — temple gravedigger *(Mountain Dwarf)*

**Read:** the guilty-looking inside man to the grave-robbery. **Voice:** surly, drink-slurred, won't meet your eye. **Wants:** to keep his old sin buried.

**Hiding:** years ago he **took a cut to let Skein dig in the consecrated ground — including Mira's grave.** Silent ever since from fear and guilt.

**Free (no roll):** he keeps the burial yard; he's twitchy about the older graves; Brother Anselm "frets too much."

**Gated:**
- *The grave-robbery* — **Charisma (Intimidation) 15** or a **bribe (~30g + Charisma 10)** → *"Aye — I looked the other way. Skein paid me. And one of the ones he took… it was the perfumer's little girl. He said a gentleman wanted her moved, real quiet."* (→ shortcut straight to motive: **the flower-man had Mira dug up**)
- *Mira's grave is empty* — B&E the ossuary / records vault (**Stealth/Investigation 15**), or Crale confirms → the grave holds no child. (→ Conclusion 4)

**Won't give:** he doesn't know what Florian *did* with her — only that "a gentleman" paid to move her. He'll name Skein fast to save his own skin.

**Dialogue —**
- *Cornered:* "I've a family. You don't know what it's like, the things you do for coin in this quarter. …Fine. Skein. Talk to Skein. And aye — one was a child. The fever year. A gentleman wanted her gone, quiet-like. I never asked which gentleman. I didn't *want* to know."

**Points to:** **Skein**, and "the gentleman who wanted the child moved."

## Nessa — unlicensed flower-girl *(Fairy)* — **the buried witness**

**Read:** the frightened innocent holding the cleanest clue. **Voice:** small, quick, watching the street for the Watch. **Wants:** to not be arrested or shaken down again.

**Hiding:** only that she sells without a license and **Roon shakes her down** — which is why she won't go to the Watch with what she saw.

**Free (once she feels safe — kindness, not authority):** she **saw the flower-cart on the high terrace before dawn**, the morning after a killing — *"the nice flower-man's cart, I think, the one from the big garden."* She told no one because the Watch frightens her.

**Gated:**
- *Reach her at all* — she **flees authority/Intimidation**; **Charisma (Persuasion) 10 with warmth**, a kindness, or coming via the Witherrows → she talks. (→ Conclusion 4, clean)
- *(If Roon's racket is broken first, she comes forward freely.)*

**Won't give:** nothing held back once safe — she's just scared, not secretive.

**Dialogue —**
- *Reassured:* "You're not Watch? …I saw a cart. Up the top terraces, before light, the morning after the poor old sweet-man died. Pretty cart. Flowers. I think it was the garden-man's. I didn't tell — the Watch'd only take my basket and worse."

**Points to:** the **dawn flower-cart** → Florian. The single cleanest opportunity clue, gated only behind *being kind.*

## Skein — the resurrection-man *(Goblin)* — **the linchpin**

**Read:** the knot that ties the two trails together. **Voice:** furtive, transactional, sells anyone for the right price or the wrong threat. **Wants:** coin, and to not swing for grave-robbing.

**Hiding:** years ago **"the flower-man" paid him to dig a child's body from the temple ground and deliver it quietly** — and he **sold the grave-goods to Castellan.** This *separates the trails*: Castellan bought trinkets; **Florian commissioned the body.**

**Free (for coin or under threat):** he digs, he sells, he asks no questions — "bad for business."

**Gated:**
- *The Mira job* — **bribe (~50g)**, **Intimidation 15**, or leverage from Crale/Castellan → *"The flower-man. Sael. Paid me triple to lift a little girl from the temple ground — the fever year — and bring her to his shop in a flower-crate. Said it was his own kin, wanted her home. I sold the baubles on to the Vane fellow. The body? Took it to the perfumery. Never asked why. You don't, in my trade."*

**Won't give:** he didn't go *inside* the conservatory and doesn't know about the murders — only the body delivery. But that one fact is the **motive nailed to Florian.**

**Dialogue —**
- *Threatened:* "Easy, easy — I'm a businessman. You want a name? Names cost. …The flower-man. Sael. He wanted a child moved, years back, hush-hush. Paid like a prince. To his *shop*, of all places. I figured grief. People do strange things with grief."

**Points to:** **Florian Sael, by name** — the breakthrough that doesn't require breaking into the conservatory.

# AUTHORITY — *access, pressure, and walls*

## Sergeant Halloran — the Watch *(Human)*

**Read:** the decent, drowning sergeant who wants an easy answer. **Voice:** tired, harried, grateful for help, defensive about his failures. **Wants:** the killing stopped and the festival calmed — ideally by tomorrow.

**Free (no roll):** the three names, sites, the coin/keepsake/perfume pattern, the Briar Bride theory, his cells and the bodies. That he's "this close" to arresting Dama Vesh because the district demands it.

**Gated:**
- *Deputize the party / full access* — **Charisma (Persuasion) 10** or the magistrate's writ → run of the district.
- *Hold off arresting Dama Vesh* — **Persuasion 15** ("give me till Midsummer") → he waits, nervously.
- *A warrant to search Florian* — **Persuasion 15 + Conclusion 1 and one of 2/3/4** → he'll cross a beloved man *only* with that; otherwise refuses.
- *Back a public accusation of Florian* — needs the **Daughter-Rose / a confession**; no roll substitutes.

**Won't give:** he won't move on Florian (whom he likes) without hard proof — that's the institutional wall, with Vell behind it.

**Dialogue —**
- *First meeting:* "Gods, the magistrate actually sent someone. Take whatever you need — the file, the cells, the bodies at the temple. Just… end it. Three weeks, and the only name I've got is a woman the whole quarter already hates."
- *Asked to cross Florian:* "*Sael?* The man crowns the festival. He sends soup to the Witherrows. Bring me something I can hold in my hand, or I'll not so much as knock on his door."

**Points to:** access, the warrant, and (reluctantly) the brakes on the mob.

## Brother Anselm — the temple *(Protector Aasimar)*

**Read:** the kindly priest hiding a shame, not a crime. **Voice:** gentle, weary, anxious. **Wants:** to atone for a failure on holy ground.

**Hiding:** he knows **Mira's grave was robbed years ago** and buried the scandal out of shame and fear; he half-suspects Crale.

**Free (no roll):** full help with the records; the bodies; the **death-record of Mira Sael** (fever, Midsummer, eight years gone, "her father never recovered"); the Midsummer remembrance.

**Gated:**
- *The empty grave* — **Charisma (Persuasion) 15** (ease his shame) or **Insight 15** on his anxiety → he admits the grave was disturbed and he hushed it; "ask Crale, gods forgive me." (→ Conclusion 4 + Crale/Skein)

**Won't give:** he doesn't know *who* or *why* — only that a child's grave was violated and he failed to stop it.

**Dialogue —**
- *Records:* "Mira Sael. Eight years. The fever took half our little ones that Midsummer — but Florian… he never lit a candle, never grieved aloud. He just went quiet, and grew roses."
- *The grave (eased):* "May the Mother forgive me. Her grave was opened, years past. I told no one — the scandal, on consecrated ground, in my keeping. Crale knows more than he says. I was a coward."

**Points to:** the Midsummer motive, and (under gentle pressure) the empty grave → Crale → Skein.

## Magistrate Vell — the employer *(Vedalken)*

**Read:** the smooth political wall; the reason an accusation must be airtight. **Voice:** measured, cool, image-conscious; never raises his voice. **Wants:** the case closed *quietly* before it stains the Bloomfeast (and his name).

**Free (no roll):** the commission (200g now, 200g on a confession or a body); the writ; the pressure to be discreet.

**Gated:**
- *Permission to publicly accuse / arrest a beloved citizen (Florian)* — **Charisma (Persuasion) 20** *and* hard proof (the Daughter-Rose, Skein's testimony, a confession); otherwise he **forbids it** and leans the party toward "the obvious Witherrows woman."
- *More time / resources* — **Persuasion 15** → grudging.

**Won't give:** a free hand against Florian on suspicion alone — he'll protect the festival's name over the truth until forced. He is a *clock and a wall*, not a suspect.

**Dialogue —**
- *Briefing:* "Three deaths in three weeks, and a city of a hundred thousand arriving for the Bloomfeast. Find me an answer — quietly. The Verdance cannot become a place people are *afraid* to visit."
- *Party names Florian without proof:* "Florian Sael. You wish me to arrest the man who *crowns the festival* — on a rose and a feeling. Bring me certainty, or bring me the Witherrows woman, and let us all sleep."

**Points to:** the bar the party must clear — and, unwittingly, why being *right* isn't enough without *proof*.

# THE VICTIMS — *(dead; their facts seed the pattern)*

Players gather these from witnesses, the bodies (Medicine 15), and the families — **freely, not behind a single gate.**

- **Lysenne** *(Satyr)* — actress/singer, Tea-Gardens. Taken: **a pearl earring (her mother's).** Radiant, beloved; close with Florian; feuding with Ysolde (knew the attar secret); a secret sweetheart; refused Aldric.
- **Old Pateros** *(Stout Halfling)* — confectioner, Bloommarket fountain. Taken: **a locket (his late wife's).** Everyone's grandfather; gave sweets to the market children.
- **Castan** *(Eladrin)* — the festival's Garland-Prince, foot of the Vermilion Garden. Taken: **the silver garland-pin of his crowning.** Golden, adored; Grenn robbed his corpse before raising the alarm.

**The shared truth (the pattern):** all three were *the warmest, most-loved people in the Verdance*; each **spoke warmly with Florian within days of dying** (he gave them blooms, asked what they treasured); each **drained through one fine curved cut**, **drenched in his perfume**, **missing the one keepsake they loved most**, **a vermilion rose laid on the breast.** That pattern — radiance + a Florian conversation + the rose — is the spine of the case.
