# The Forge of Fury — Cast & Dialogue

Voice, wants, and ready-to-read lines for the people (and ghost, and dragon) of Khundrukar. Companion to [adventure.md](adventure.md). Read a line, don't recite the block. Everyone here wants something, and — per the house rules ([../../rules/16-co-gm-guide.md](../../rules/16-co-gm-guide.md)) — the NPCs will wander off-topic, ask the party things, and refuse to info-dump on command. Give fragments; let the table assemble the picture.

---

## Halvard Kess — the patron (surface, before the delve)

A weathered caravan-master out of Bridghaven; lost two mule-trains and a nephew on the north road this spring.

*Want:* The road open again. And, quieter, someone to blame that isn't himself for sending the boy north.
*Weird thing:* He keeps his dead nephew's tally-ledger on him and touches it when he talks numbers.

**Voice:** blunt, tired, generous when it costs him nothing, tight when it costs him coin.

- **The hook:** *"Something's holed up in the old dwarf-hold — Khundrukar, the maps call it. Orcs on the raids, sure, but there's a thing they answer to, deeper down. I don't need the whole story. I need the road safe. Two hundred now, three hundred when you walk back out. And whatever old dwarf-steel you drag up is yours to keep — Durgeddin's make, if the tales aren't all lies. That stuff's worth a caravan."*
- **If pressed on the "thing below":** *"Traders' talk. Something big. Something in the water. I sent Yorrin Ashfell up there a fortnight past to poke at the history of it — dwarf, scholar, wouldn't shut up about black blades. Haven't heard from him since. You find him, there's a bonus in it."* **(Plants Yorrin.)**
- **If they haggle:** touches the ledger. *"...Two-fifty on return. That's my nephew's route you're clearing. Don't make me count higher than that."*

## Yorrin Ashfell — the prisoner & the key (the Glitterhame)

A dwarf antiquarian, mid-years, soot-stained and scab-kneed, caged by the troglodytes for a week. **The single richest source of the three win-button clues** — but he doles them out between complaints, questions, and terror, not in a lecture.

*Want:* Out, alive. And — he'd die before admitting it — to be *the dwarf who found Durgeddin's forge and came back.* His whole life bends toward that.
*Weird thing:* Fearless about orcs, trogs, the dark — but he **flinches from running water** and won't look downhill. He's seen the dragon come up the streams.

**Voice:** fast, wry, over-talkative, cracks jokes when scared (which is always, down here). Warms fast to anyone who cuts him loose.

### On being freed
> *"Oh, bless you, bless every one of you — no, don't mind the smell, that's the trogs, not me, mostly. A week in a cage of spit and stalagmites. Yorrin Ashfell, antiquary, at your considerable service. You're Kess's lot? Ha — he does pay when a body actually comes back. Right. You want to know what's in this mountain before it kills you. Sit. No — don't sit, we haven't the time. Walk and listen."*

### The three clues (spread them; let players pull on threads)

**Clue 1 — Durgeddin & the black steel (→ win-button #1):**
> *"This was Khundrukar. Durgeddin the Black — greatest smith of the age and the maddest. Orcs butchered his clan at Dunhollow when he was young, so he came here and forged. Just... forged. Orc-killing blades, black as a shut mine, keen as a grudge. Hundreds of them, the tale says — an armory for a war he never got to fight. The orcs found him first. Killed the last of his people over steel that killed nothing."*
> — and, lower — *"One of those black blades cut the dragon once. When she was small. She's grown, but she hasn't forgotten. You put black steel in front of her, she remembers being hurt. That's worth more than a spell, that fear."*

**Clue 2 — Nightscale & how she fights (→ the boss's gimmick):**
> *"There's a young black wyrm in the flooded deep. The trogs feed her, the gray dwarves pay her rent. She's the reason I stopped at these caves and thanked the trogs for the cage, near enough. She fights from the water — dives, heals in the deep, comes up spitting acid, dives again before you can land a blow. You'll die of old age trying to pin her while she's got her lake to hide in."*

**Clue 3 — the waterworks (→ win-button #2):**
> *"But she's only got her lake because the dwarves let the water sit there. Dwarves never let water do as it pleases — there are sluice-gates, great chained wheels, built to hold back the Mere's seep into the deep. The controls are on the forge-level. My grandsire's notes swore the old smiths could* drain *that lower cavern in minutes, if the works still turn. Drain the lake..."* — he finally smiles — *"...and your dragon's a fish on a dock."*

### Questions he asks the party (play him as a person)
- *"Did Kess send my bonus up with you, or is he holding it hostage against my safe return? ...That's a yes to the hostage, isn't it."*
- *"Is Great Ulfe dead? The big one? Good. He had a black axe — did you take it? Please tell me you took it. That's — do you understand what you're holding?"*
- *"You didn't come down through the sinkhole, did you? Tell me you didn't wake the thing in the sinkhole."*

### If asked to come along
> *"Down there? To the water? No. No, I've a better idea — I'll wait in that bolt-hole with a barred door and my notes and a very small candle, and I'll translate anything you drag back up. That's the deal. I'm a scholar, not a sword. Go be heroes. I'll be alive."*

## Great Ulfe — the chieftain (Mountain Door)

Half-ogre orc, near ogre-sized, one gold-capped tusk. Doesn't parley; barely talks. Give him one or two roars and let the axe do the rest.

*Want:* To be feared. To keep his "lucky" black axe.
*Weird thing:* He treats the finest weapon in the mountain like a rabbit's foot — kisses the flat of it before a fight.

- **On sighting the party:** *"Little road-meat walked all the way up my mountain. Good. Save me the trip down."*
- **When first bloodied (a roar to his orcs):** *"UP! Up and kill, you gutless — the axe is thirsty!"*
- **He has no idea what the axe is.** If a PC name-drops Durgeddin or "black steel," he just laughs — the word means nothing to him. That's the point: humanize the tragedy by how *little* the thief understands the theft.

## The Duergar & Xazandra the Overseer (the Foundry)

Gray dwarves, colorless and joyless, salvaging Durgeddin's forge for profit. **Not fanatics — negotiators.** The party can fight them, buy them off, or point them at the dragon.

*Xazandra wants:* The blade-cache out and sold, the tribute to Nightscale ended, her crew alive (in that order — the crew's negotiable).
*Weird thing:* She talks about the shade of Durgeddin the way you'd talk about bad weather — a nuisance to work around, not a horror. Two centuries of grief, and she's *bored* by it.

**Voice:** flat, transactional, unimpressed. Never raises her voice; that's what makes her unnerving.

- **Opening, if the party doesn't ambush:** *"Surface-folk. Of course. Stop there. We've no quarrel — we're hauling steel, not fighting adventurers for free. You want the orcs? Dead already killing themselves upstairs. You want the wyrm? *Please* — take the wyrm, we're sick of feeding her. What we want is this forge emptied and a clear road down. Walk away, or make me a better offer than a fight."*
- **The deal on the table:** *"Half the cache. You take half, we take half, nobody bleeds, and I'll even tell you where the drain-wheels are. Or we do this the loud way and wake the thing in the water for all of us. Your call. Count fast."* **(A deal costs the party some blades but buys the Foundry fight for free — and she'll still spill the sluice clue.)**
- **When bloodied (fleeing):** *"Crew — the barge! We are NOT dying for dwarf-scrap!"* — and she bolts down the water-channel toward the lake. *(Foreshadow: she runs toward the dragon rather than face the party — because to a duergar, the dragon is the known quantity.)*
- **At the lake, if she fled there:** she'll fight beside Nightscale to save herself — or, the instant the party is clearly winning, switch: *"Wyrm's finished. I was never here. We never met."*

## Durgeddin's Shade — the broken heart (the Foundry)

The tragic core of the whole delve. Two hundred years of grief in the shape of a broad old smith, hammering a cold anvil forever, guarding blades for an army of ghosts. **Not a monster — a wound.** Run this as the emotional climax *before* the mechanical one. He speaks old dwarvish; Yorrin or a Lore check translates.

*Want:* His people back. The muster to come. The war he armed to finally be fought — and, underneath, never spoken: to be *allowed to stop.*
*Weird thing:* He does not see the duergar at all. They are not orc-kind; they are beneath his grief. He sees only weapons, and orcs, and the kin who never came.

**Voice:** slow, deep, certain, and sorrowful. He is *sure* he is doing right — the same terrible certainty as the Pale Shepherd and Florian. Do not play him as raving; play him as a man who has been waiting so long he's forgotten waiting isn't living.

### When shown a black blade (his hammer stops)
> *"...You carry Dunhollow-Avenged. I know that steel — I drew the grain of it myself, wept into the quench. Then the muster has come. At last. At LAST."*
> *(He turns, and his voice cracks two hundred years wide.)*
> *"Where is the army, kinsman? I forged for all of you — every hand that would ever lift a blade against the orc-holds. Hundreds. I have hundreds. Where are the others? Where did everyone... where did they go? Why does no one ever* come*?"*

### If the party tells him the truth (the brutal kindness)
Let a player say it — the war is over, the clan is two hundred years dead and at rest, there is no army, there was never going to be. Land it as a real Charisma moment (DC by the fiction, not a gotcha). If it lands:
> *(The hammer lowers. For a long moment the only sound is water.)*
> *"...Two hundred years. I kept forging so I would not have to hear that. So I would not have to set it down."*
> *(He looks at the black blade in their hands.)*
> *"Then take it. Take them all. Let it kill something at last — the wyrm in the deep will do, she who came up my streams and ate what the orcs left. Strike her with my steel and she will remember the day it bit her small. Go. And when it's done..."* — a pause that is almost a smile — *"...let the fire go out. I am so tired, kinsman. Let it go out."*
> *(He sets the hammer on the cold anvil, and is gone. One chosen black blade in the room hums, briefly, with a cold light: Dunhollow-Avenged, blessed at last on something worth the killing.)*

### If the party just takes the blades and leaves
He lets them. He keeps hammering. As they go, without turning:
> *"Arm yourselves, kinsmen. The muster will come. It always... it always comes."*
> *(No peace. The grief stays lit. The table will feel it.)*

### If provoked into a fight
Only if the party raises steel to him or threatens the forge. Play it as tragic, not triumphant — every hammer-blow is a sob. (Stat block in [adventure.md](adventure.md).) Steer away from this; it's the worst of the three endings and the least interesting.

## Nightscale — the boss (the Black Lake)

Young black dragon: sleek, cruel, vain, and *afraid of exactly one thing.* She covers the fear with mockery. Play her purring and confident right up until black steel or drained mud takes it away.

*Want:* Her hoard, her lake, and to never feel the black steel bite again.
*Weird thing:* She *talks* — young dragons love the sound of their own menace. Let her banter, boast, and bargain. It makes her human enough to make her fall land.

**Voice:** silk over teeth. Cruel comedy. Drops the act, hard, when frightened.

- **On the party's entrance (eyeing the black blade):** *"You brought me a* toy*. How thoughtful. I'll have it back the way I got the first one — out of a dead dwarf's hand. Do you know, none of them ever bring it to me on purpose. You're my first volunteers."*
- **Breathing acid:** *"Open wide."*
- **When a black blade first bites her (the crack shows):** *(she shrieks — too high, too young, all the silk gone for one instant)* *"— NO. Not that. Not — where did you FIND —"* *(then she dives, and comes up snarling, and the mockery is thinner now.)*
- **As the lake drains around her:** *"What — what are you — the water — what did you DO —"* *(A young dragon flopping on the mud is not a menace. She knows it. Let her know it out loud.)*
- **Stranded, black steel closing in (she breaks — pick one):**
  - **Beg:** *"Wait — WAIT — the hoard, take the hoard, take all of it, I'll go, I'll fly north and you'll never — please, not the black steel, not again —"*
  - **Bargain:** *"There are worse things than me under this mountain, little dwarf-friends. Let me live and I'll tell you what the gray ones are really digging toward. That's worth more than my hide, isn't it? Isn't it?"* **(A campaign hook, if they take it.)**
  - **Bolt:** she throws herself up a drain-tunnel in a spray of mud, and is gone — grown, humiliated, and now she knows their faces. *(A future grudge with wings.)*

Let the players decide whether a beaten wyrm gets to live. Both endings are good. Killing her closes the story; sparing her opens a bigger one.

## Quick emotional map (for the GM)

- **Kess** — the ordinary grief the party is *paid* to fix (a dead nephew, a closed road).
- **Yorrin** — the friend; comic relief that carries the plot. Keep him alive; he's your Bridghaven hook engine.
- **Ulfe** — the thief who doesn't know what he stole. Irony, not tragedy.
- **Xazandra** — the pragmatist; the mirror that says *"none of this grief is my problem."* Makes the shade's grief hit harder by contrast.
- **Durgeddin** — the real heart: grief that armed a war and forgot how to stop. The party's kindness (or its absence) is the adventure's true climax.
- **Nightscale** — the fear made flesh; young enough to break. The mechanical climax that pays off every clue the party gathered.

Give fragments. Let them build it. Bring the black blades home.
