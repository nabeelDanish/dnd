# The Falling Spire

> A one-shot for **three level-20 heroes** (the pregens in [pregens.md](pregens.md)). Master-tier, mythic stakes, one ticking clock. Runs in about **3–4 hours.** Built on the lean d20 rules in [/rules](../../rules).

## The premise

For a thousand years the **Aurorspire** has hung in the sky over **Bridghaven** — an elven sky-citadel, kept aloft by a captive: **Khaltesh the Storm-Titan**, a primordial of lightning and ruin, bound into the spire's core. Its caged fury is the engine; its struggle is the lift.

This morning, the seal cracked.

Khaltesh is tearing free, and as the binding fails the Aurorspire is **falling** — a mile of ancient stone dropping toward a city of fifty thousand. The party has roughly **one hour** before impact. They must board the falling spire, fight down to the **Heartcage** at its heart, and either **re-forge the binding** (Seraphine's foresight can thread it) or **gamble everything** on shattering Khaltesh outright — before the Aurorspire turns Bridghaven into a crater.

> **The clock is the adventure.** There are three acts; each costs the party time. Narrate the descent constantly — the horizon tilting, the city's rooftops swelling below, bells of panic rising up to meet them. Don't track minutes on a stopwatch; track *dread*. If the party dawdles, the ground gets closer.

## Opening — the leap *(everyone)*

The party is in Bridghaven when the spire's shadow begins to *grow*. A skyship captain, an old contact of Lirael's, will run one suicide pass beneath the falling citadel — close enough to jump. Three legends, one open hatch, the wind screaming.

> Read-aloud: *"The Aurorspire fills the whole sky now, close enough to see the gardens spilling off its broken edges like water. The captain screams over the wind — 'I can give you ONE pass, that's IT —' The hull bucks. The jump point is a shattered causeway, rushing up. Go now, or there is no now."*

Getting aboard is a **group check** (Athletics or Acrobatics, Hard 15 — Lirael's Keen Senses / Acrobatics shine; Vorrik can be hauled across by an ally on a success). Failure isn't death — it's *taking the long way*, a hit of the clock, and a bad first position in Act 1.

## Act 1 — The Shattered Causeway

A crumbling sky-bridge across open air, the city wheeling far below. The spire's escaping essence has congealed into **Storm-Wraiths** — knots of loosed lightning that lash anything alive. **2–3 wraiths**, more arriving each round the party lingers.

**The hazard:** the causeway is **breaking apart**. Each round, the GM collapses one 2-square section (telegraphed the round before). A creature on a collapsing section must move clear or fall — a falling PC of this tier survives (catch a ledge, *Misty Step*, the skyship swoops — be generous; this is spectacle, not a death trap), but loses their next action climbing back.

**Spotlights:**
- **Lirael** — flyers over open air with no cover: *Deadeye* + a marked target = wraiths deleted before they close. **Evasion** shrugs off their lightning entirely.
- **Vorrik** — plant on a stable slab; **Bulwark +2** turns the bridge into a fortress; wraiths that whiff trigger **Riposte Mastery**.
- **Seraphine** — *Haste* on Lirael, or split the wraith pack from the party with **Wall of Force**. (Her **radiant/necrotic** resist doesn't cover lightning — she stays back.)

> **Storm-Wraith — Tier 3 · Threat 7**
> **HP** 60 · **Armor** 17 · **Mind** 14 · **Spirit** 15 · **Charisma** 12 · **Speed** fly 10
> **Attack:** *Lightning Lash* `+10 vs. Armor → 3d8` lightning, Range Near.
> **Special:** *Storm Form* — resistance to physical damage; moves through creatures and the gaps in the bridge freely. *Earthing* — if it ends its turn touching metal armor, its next Lash is at advantage.
> **Notes:** No self-preservation; flung-off essence of a titan. Arrives in twos. Vulnerable to being *grounded* — a creature that grapples or pins one (or a *Wall of Force* box) ends its Storm Form until it breaks free.

Clear the causeway and the door to the Gearwell grinds open — slow, because the spire's machinery is dying.

## Act 2 — The Gearwell

The spire's heart-machinery: vast slow-turning levitation rings inscribed with **binding-runes**, half of them dark. Guarding the inner stair stands the **Aurorspire Sentinel**, a construct that has stood here a thousand years and does not understand that the war it was built for is over.

**Two ways through — let the party choose, under the clock:**

1. **Fight the Sentinel.** Brutal and slow. It's a wall of iron with magic resistance — a straight slugfest that eats time and HP. Vorrik can hold it (it can't move him); Lirael's mark + physical shots chew it (it has no physical resistance); Seraphine's spells fight uphill (magic resistance), so she's better healing and using **Foresight** to cancel its prone-slams.

2. **Re-light the runes** *(skill challenge — the clever path).* Bring three dark levitation rings back online to *slow the fall* and put the Sentinel to sleep (it stands down once the spire's systems read "stable"). A **3-success-before-2-failures** challenge, escalating targets:
   - **Read the rune-logic** — Arcana or Investigation, Hard 15 *(Seraphine, Vorrik's Lore)*.
   - **Re-seat a cracked ring** — Athletics or Crafting, Heroic 20 *(Vorrik's Might)*.
   - **Balance the failing lattice** — a Mind or Spirit check, Heroic 20 *(Seraphine; or anyone with Tactics/Insight)*.
   - Each **failure** = the Sentinel gets a free slam at whoever's exposed, and the fall lurches (a clock hit).

> **Aurorspire Sentinel — Tier 3 · Threat 13**
> **HP** 200 · **Armor** 20 · **Mind** 8 · **Spirit** 12 · **Charisma** 6 · **Speed** 6
> **Attack:** *Iron Slam* `+11 vs. Armor → 3d10 + 6`; on a hit the target is knocked **Prone**.
> **Special:** Sentinel. *Magic Resistance* — advantage on all defenses vs. spells, and spell attacks against it are at disadvantage. *Immune* to poison, charm, sleep, frightened. *Stand Down* — if the spire's runes are restored (the skill challenge), it returns to its alcove and stops fighting.
> **Notes:** Mindless duty. Targets whoever last damaged it. It cannot pursue past the Gearwell.

Either way, the inner stair opens onto the core. The light down there is wrong — too blue, too alive.

## Act 3 — The Heartcage (the boss)

A cathedral-sized sphere of bound lightning. **Khaltesh** is *half-out* — one arm of storm already free, the binding-runes around it screaming and snapping one by one. Below, through the floor's crystal, the rooftops of Bridghaven are close enough to count.

> Read-aloud: *"It turns what passes for a face toward you, and the sound it makes is not language but you understand it anyway: relief. After a thousand years, something finally came to be here at the end. 'GOOD,' the storm says. 'WITNESSES.'"*

### The two win conditions — make the party choose

- **RE-FORGE THE BINDING (the save).** Seraphine (or any caster) channels the failing seal over **3 rounds** (each round: a Spirit or Mind check vs. 20, **Foresight can buy a reroll**), while the others keep Khaltesh off her and keep it *Half-Bound*. Succeed and Khaltesh is re-caged, the lift re-engages, **the spire stops falling.** Bridghaven lives; the titan sleeps another age. *(Bittersweet — the cage is cruelty, and it will crack again someday.)*
- **SHATTER KHALTESH (the gamble).** Drop it to **0 HP**. The binding explodes; the freed storm *blasts upward* instead of down (a legend-worthy roll — let them earn it), and the now-inert spire must still be stopped from falling: a final desperate **group check** (Heroic 20, advantage if any runes were re-lit in Act 2) to ride the dead spire to a survivable crash in the harbor. *(Heroic, costly, permanent — the titan is gone for good.)*

Both are *winnable*; both should feel like they cost something. The clock makes the choice real: re-binding is safer but the titan endures; shattering frees the world of it but risks the city on one last roll.

> **KHALTESH, THE STORM-TITAN — Tier 3 · Boss · Threat ~30**
> **HP** 320 · **Armor** 19 · **Mind** 16 · **Spirit** 18 · **Charisma** 17 · **Speed** 8, fly 12
> **Attacks (multiattack):** two *Storm-Fists* `+12 vs. Armor → 4d10 + 7` each.
> *Chain Lightning* (recharge 5–6): an arc through up to 4 creatures in Near of each other, `+12 vs. each Armor → 8d6` lightning. *(Lirael's **Evasion** halves/negates this; Seraphine should **Foresight** a failed recharge or pre-empt with **Wall of Force**.)*
> **Special:**
> - *Frightful Presence* — a creature that starts its turn within Near rolls **Charisma vs. +12** or is **Frightened** 1 round. *(Vorrik's mental defenses and a Dwarf-grade will shrug it; Lirael at range avoids it.)*
> - *Half-Bound* — **while at least one binding-rune holds, one of Khaltesh's two Storm-Fists each round is at disadvantage.** The runes hold as long as the party is *re-forging* (win condition 1) or until Khaltesh spends a full action to rip one free (it has 3 left). Smashing the runes frees its full fury — a tempting, terrible accelerant for the *shatter* path.
> - *Legendary Surge* — at the **end of each round**, Khaltesh moves up to half its Speed and makes one extra *Storm-Fist*. It does not stop.
> **Notes:** Ancient, vast, and — under the rage — *tired*. It is not evil; it is a caged animal at the end of its patience. Play the relief, the exhaustion, the strange courtesy toward those strong enough to end this one way or another. A clever party can *talk to it* mid-fight (Charisma vs. its 17) — not to pacify it, but to learn that it will fly *away*, not down, if simply freed. That's the seed of mercy.

**Boss tactics:** It opens on the squishiest cluster with Chain Lightning. It will try to peel toward **Seraphine** — Vorrik's job is to make that impossible (body-block, Immovable, Riposte). Keep it *Half-Bound* as long as you can; the disadvantaged Fist is the difference between a downed PC and a dead one. Lirael marks it turn one and never stops; with capstone Hunter's Mark + Shadowstrike she's doing 30–40 a round. Seraphine's **Foresight twice/round** is the MVP button: cancel a recharged Chain Lightning, force a crit-on-Vorrik into a miss, or yank a death save back.

## Aftermath

- **Re-forged:** the Aurorspire shudders to a halt a thousand feet above the spires of Bridghaven and begins, groaning, to climb. The city does not even know how close it came. The party are anonymous saviors — which suits some of them fine. Khaltesh sleeps. *Someone* will have to watch the seal now.
- **Shattered:** a column of freed storm punches into the heavens and rolls away to sea, and the dead spire crashes into the harbor in a wave that floods the docks but breaks no towers. Bridghaven crawls out soaked and alive. The Aurorspire is a reef now. The sky is, for the first time in a thousand years, *empty* — and Khaltesh is free somewhere out over the ocean, owing three mortals a debt it may one day remember.

**Rewards** (master tier — they're already legends, so make these *story*, not stat bumps): a shard of the binding-crystal (a one-use *Counterspell* against any single effect, no mana); the gratitude (or fearful awe) of a saved city; and — if they freed it — a storm on the horizon that always seems to be watching their ship.

## GM notes

- **Difficulty by feel, not budget.** The encounter math ([13-encounters-and-rewards.md](../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md)) was tuned for the apprentice game; at level 20 it under-counts. These blocks are built to *genuinely threaten three legends* — Khaltesh especially can drop a PC. If the fight is running soft, lean on **Legendary Surge**, ripping runes free (ending Half-Bound), and a fresh pair of **Storm-Wraiths** boiling up out of the core. If it's too brutal, let the runes hold an extra round and have Chain Lightning miss its recharge.
- **The clock over the kill.** Reward speed and cleverness. A party that re-lit runes in Act 2 gets advantage on the climax; a party that wasted time in Act 1 finds the rooftops *very* close in Act 3.
- **Charisma defense matters here** — Frightful Presence targets it, and the four-defense math is live. Vorrik (Cha 13) and a focused Seraphine resist; warn players it's in play.
- **Let them be mythic.** These are level-20 heroes. When Lirael one-shots a wraith from across the open sky, or Vorrik plants himself in a titan's path and *does not move*, or Seraphine reaches out and tells fate *no* — slow down and let it land. That's the whole point of running the top of the ladder.
