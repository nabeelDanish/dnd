# One-Shot: The Pale Gate

A single-session adventure for **four level-3 characters**. Play time: **~4–5 hours**. Pre-made characters are in [characters.md](characters.md).

This is a harder, longer one-shot than [The Bell of Ashvane](../the-bell-of-ashvane/adventure.md) — five fights of escalating difficulty, no full rest until the end, and a dimension-crossing structure. It's built for players who already know the system. The combat arc is deliberately punishing; read the **Survival & pacing** section before you run it.

## The pitch

Two days' ride inland from Bridghaven, on the high peat moors, sits the hamlet of **Hollowmere** — sixty souls who fish the black mere, cut peat, and keep the moor-lamps lit so travelers don't drown in the bog at night. Three weeks ago, Hollowmere went silent. A wool-merchant found it empty: doors open, meals on the tables, every hearth cold, every lantern in the village *gone*.

The reeve of Bridghaven has hired the party to find out what happened to Hollowmere — and, if anyone lives, to bring them home. **150 gold now, 150 on return**, and the gratitude of a frightened district.

## The truth (GM only)

A century ago, Hollowmere's lamplighter — **Hesper Loom** — walked onto the moor in a great autumn fog to light the bog-lamps and never came back. The villagers searched, found nothing, and built a tall iron **Lamp-post** on the green in his memory. Every Hollow's Eve since, they kept one flame burning on it for him, "so he can find his way home in the dark."

He found his way home.

Hesper did not die on the moor. He bargained with the thing that lives in the fog — **the Hollow**, a grey, fading mirror of the world where forgotten things go — so that *his* light would never go out. The price: he tends the Hollow's lamps now. And a lamp needs fuel. He calls himself **the Pale Shepherd**, and he believes, with terrible sincerity, that he is being *kind*: he harvests whole villages, draws each soul into a lantern, and keeps them "safe from the dark" forever in the Hollow — instead of letting them suffer and die out in the world.

Three weeks ago, on the new moon, the memorial Lamp-post guttered fully dark for the first time in a hundred years. That darkness is a **door**. Hesper walked through it, led the sixty villagers of Hollowmere into the green by his lantern-light, and took them home.

Their souls now hang as **soul-lanterns** in his hall. They are not yet lost — but the Hollow is *eating* them, one at a time. The party has until the village's central soul, the **Hearth-lantern**, gutters out. Then the sixty are gone forever and the gate seals.

## The shape of the session

The adventure runs as a fixed sequence of beats. The combat escalates at every step.

| Beat | Type | Threat | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Combat** — ambush | 8 (~66%) | Hollowmere village |
| 2 | **Investigation** | — | Hollowmere village |
| — | *Portal* | — | The Pale Gate |
| 3 | **Combat** — wave 1 | 10 (~83%) | The Ashen Threshold |
| 4 | **Combat** — wave 2 | 14 (~117%) | The Ashen Threshold |
| 5 | **Movement & investigation** | — | The Bone Road |
| 6 | **Puzzle** | — | The Lantern Vault |
| 7 | **Combat** — guardians | 15 (~125%) | The Tallow Hall doors |
| 8 | **Boss** | ~24 (200%) | The Tallow Hall |

Budget reference: a four-player level-3 party has an encounter budget of **12 threat points** (party level × players). 100% is a standard fight. See [../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md](../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md).

## The clock — "The Guttering"

There is no literal time-of-day clock here; the pressure is the **dying of the lanterns**. The Hollow consumes the trapped villagers steadily, and faster when the party stalls.

Run it like this:
- The party starts with **all 60 villagers still savable.**
- Each time the party **takes a long pause** — drinks from the Stillwater Pool (a full rest, see below), spends a long time on the puzzle, or otherwise dawdles — **a handful of soul-lanterns gutter out.** Narrate it: a lantern on the Bone Road winks dark; a face in the glass goes still.
- Mechanically you don't need to track an exact number. Track a **mood**: as the party lingers, the Hollow gets hungrier, the fog gets thicker, and you describe fewer and fewer lights. If they sprint, most of Hollowmere comes home. If they crawl, they save a fraction.
- **The hard floor:** if the party rests at the Stillwater Pool, **1d6 + 4 villagers are lost forever.** That is the cost of the rest. Tell them this *before* they decide.

This is the resource-drama lever from [../../rules/14-gm-guide.md](../../rules/14-gm-guide.md): the rest that saves the party costs the souls they came to save. Make them feel the trade.

## Survival & pacing (read this)

Five escalating fights with one optional rest is a brutal adventuring day, even at level 3. Keep the party alive without trivializing the threat:

- **They start fresh** — full HP, full mana, all once-per-fight abilities. The pre-made characters are built tough for exactly this (the Orc's Savage Reserves, the Priest's Spirit Guardians, the Mage's fire vs. wax).
- **Healing is seeded along the route**, not handed out at the end. Listed in each scene: potions in the village, a mana draught and the Stillwater respite in the Hollow, freed souls that heal in the boss fight. Don't withhold them.
- **Wounded enemies break.** The Hollow's lesser things (husks, hounds, wisps) are cowardly or mindless; when a fight is clearly lost for them, melt them away. New monsters arriving is fair; buffing the ones on the table is not. See [../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md](../../rules/13-encounters-and-rewards.md).
- **Radiant and fire are the answers.** Telegraph it constantly: the Hollow's creatures *flinch* from light. Sacred Flame, Spirit Guardians, the Priest's Light cantrip, the Mage's Burning Hands all do extra work here. Reward players who notice.
- **If the party is getting wrecked**, the Stillwater Pool rest is your pressure valve — but it costs souls, so the players choose it, you don't impose it. If they refuse to rest and are dying anyway, have a wounded Hollow Knight retreat, or let a freed soul shield a PC for one hit. Don't fudge dice; change the board.
- **If the party is steamrolling**, the Pale Shepherd notices them sooner — wave 2 arrives in force, and Call the Flock works overtime in the boss fight.

A PC *can* die here. That's the intended stakes. But signal danger early (the village is a graveyard; the soul-lanterns beg; the Shepherd has a name people whisper) so any death is earned, not a gotcha.

## How to run this

You're the GM. Reference the rules ([../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md](../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md) through [../../rules/15-quickstart.md](../../rules/15-quickstart.md)) as needed. If you have an AI assistant (Claude) at the table, you can ask them to roll for monsters/NPCs, track combat state and the Guttering, look up rules, read NPC dialogue, and suggest monster tactics. The rulings are yours.

### The battlemap

A pre-built map is included: [map.json](map.json). It covers **two planes stacked vertically**:

- **South (positive Y):** Hollowmere village — the green, the Lamp-post (the gate), the houses, the chapel, the well, and the cellar where the survivor hides.
- **North / below (negative Y):** the Hollow — the Ashen Threshold (waves 1 & 2), the Stillwater Pool, the Bone Road with its soul-lanterns, the Lantern Vault (puzzle), and the Tallow Hall with the Great Lantern and the boss.

NPC and enemy tokens are placed. **Player tokens are NOT included** — drop them at the **PLAYERS START** marker (the pink square on the south road) when the session begins.

To load it:

1. Open `map-tool/index.html` in a browser (DM view).
2. In the toolbar, click **Import map…**.
3. Pick `adventures/the-pale-gate/map.json`.
4. Hollowmere should appear near the screen center; the Hollow extends north (up) and far below.
5. Click **Open Player View →** to spawn the player window; mirror it to the table's screen.
6. **Turn Fog of War ON** before play (Fog tool → "Fog of War: ON"). The whole map is one continuous image, so the fog is what hides the Hollow until the party crosses the gate. Paint reveals as they explore.

To regenerate or tweak the map, edit `build-map.js` in this folder and run `node build-map.js`.

## Scene 1 — Hollowmere, and the things that came back (Combat 1)

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *You smell it before you see it: cold ash, and under that, something sweeter and worse. Hollowmere sits in a bowl of black moor, forty low stone houses around a green. Every door stands open. A child's wooden horse lies in the lane. On a table through one window, three bowls of porridge have gone to grey fur. And in the center of the green stands a tall iron lamp-post — old, ornate, its glass dark — with sixty unburnt candles heaped around its base like offerings at a grave.*
>
> *Nothing moves. Then, from the dark doorways, something does.*

The party arrives at dusk. The village is a tableau of a vanishing mid-evening: everyone gone at once, three weeks ago. Give them a moment to take it in — let it be eerie — then the ambush hits.

**The Hollow keeps a few of its servants in the world** to watch the gate. They've been waiting in the empty houses. When the party steps onto the green, the husks shamble out of the doorways and the pale hounds come loping off the moor.

### Encounter — Threat 8 (ambush)

- **4 × Hollow Husk** (Threat 1 each) — emerge from the houses on the green.
- **2 × Pale Hound** (Threat 2 each) — come in off the moor edges.

The enemies have **surprise** — they were hidden and waiting. Per [../../rules/05-combat.md](../../rules/05-combat.md), the ambushing side goes first this round; roll initiative normally from round 2. (If a PC made a point of scouting carefully before stepping onto the green, you can let a Perception check, Hard, cancel the surprise — reward caution.)

This is the easy opener. It teaches the table that **the Hollow's things flinch from light** (a husk struck by Sacred Flame recoils; the hounds shy from the Mage's Light cantrip) and that the husks are slow but swarm. Don't let a PC drop here — if one is in danger, have a hound peel off to chase a fleeing villager-thing instead.

**Stat blocks** are in the bestiary at the end of this document.

## Scene 2 — Investigation

Once the green is clear, the village is theirs to search. **Don't gate clues behind rolls** — if a player looks somewhere a clue is, they find it (see [../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md](../../rules/01-core-mechanic.md)). Rolls are only for the optional deeper reads.

What's here to find:

- **Every hearth is cold and every lantern is gone.** Forty houses, not a single lamp, candle-lantern, or hand-light anywhere — though the candles themselves are piled, unlit, at the Lamp-post. *Someone gathered all the light in the village to one place.*
- **They vanished mid-evening, all at once, three weeks ago.** Meals half-eaten, a loom still threaded, a baby's blanket in a cradle. No blood. No struggle. No bodies.
- **The Lamp-post on the green.** Tall, iron, a century old, its great glass lantern dark and cold. A bronze plate at its base: *"For Hesper Loom, lamplighter, lost to the fog. A light kept, that he may find his way home."* Around its base, the sixty unburnt candles — one, you realize, for each missing villager. *(Arcana or Investigation, Hard: the post hums with a cold, patient magic — it is not a memorial. It is a* threshold*, and it is* waiting to be lit *the wrong way.)*
- **The chapel of the Sea-Watcher** (the same coastal light-keeping faith from Bridghaven). Inside: a water-stained record of village history, and the **legend of the Lamplighter** — Hesper Loom, who walked into the autumn fog to light the bog-lamps and was never found; the village's vow to keep his flame; an old wives' warning copied in the margin in a shaking hand: *"Do not let the lamp die wholly out. On the dark of the year, a dead lamp is a door, and he is on the other side of it, and he is hungry now."*
- **The survivor.** In the cellar of a house at the edge of the green hides **Maren**, the lamplighter's apprentice — a girl of fourteen who was down a well-shaft fetching a dropped bucket when it happened, and came up to an empty village. She's been living on root-cellar stores for three weeks, too frightened to walk the two days to Bridghaven alone. (She's the only living thing in Hollowmere; the party will hear her if they search the houses.)

### Maren (the survivor)

*Want:* For someone to make it not-her-fault. To get her people back. To not be left alone again.
*Weird thing:* She talks to the unlit candles at the Lamp-post by name — she knows whose is whose.

Maren saw it from the lip of the well:

- *"The fog came up off the mere — but wrong, grey, going the wrong way, uphill. The lamps went out, one by one, house by house, like something was breathing them out. And then he was there. Tall. Thin. A coat all stitched out of grey, and a candle for a face, and a lantern on a long crook. The old lamp-post lit up — not warm, white, like the moon — and everyone... walked into it. Smiling. They just walked into the lamp and were gone. I didn't. I couldn't. I hid."*
- She knows the Lamplighter's name from the old stories: **Hesper Loom.** *"Gran said never say it on the dark of the year."*
- She knows how the lamp was *lit*: *"He lit it from his own lantern. White fire. If you put a flame to the dead lamp on a dark night — I think it opens. I think that's the door."*
- If they ask what the villagers were like — fishers, peat-cutters, mere-folk. *"We never ploughed. Nothing grows up here but heather and grief. We fished the mere and cut the black peat."* **(Note this — it's a puzzle clue.)**
- She begs them not to leave her. Reasonable options: send her down the road with a token to the next farmstead (she may refuse), hide her in the chapel (safest in-village option), or — if the players insist — bring her along (don't; the Hollow is no place for her, and you can have her sensibly refuse to enter the lamp).

### What the party should leave the village knowing

- The villagers were taken **into the Lamp-post**, which is a gate that opens when you light the dead lamp with a flame in the dark.
- The taker is **Hesper Loom, the Lamplighter** — a hundred-year-old legend, now something else.
- Wherever the villagers are, they may still be alive/savable — Maren saw them *walk*, not die.
- Hollowmere were **mere-fishers and peat-cutters, never farmers.** *(Puzzle clue. Plant it without flagging it.)*

If they're missing a piece, have Maren or the chapel record volunteer it. **Don't stall the adventure on one roll.**

### Reward in the village

- **2 × Healing Potion** (`1d8 + 2` each) in the chapel's mercy-chest, and **Captain's-courage**, a small flask of strong moor-spirits Maren offers — *one use, drink as an action to clear the Frightened condition and gain advantage on your next Spirit defense for one round* (a common magic item; it's liquid courage, half real).
- Maren can also hand over the lamplighter's old **tinderbox and a single long taper** — *the flame to open the gate.* (If the players think to light the dead lamp themselves, this is how. If they don't, the gate also opens on its own at full dark — see Scene 3.)

## Scene 3 — The Pale Gate (the portal)

When the party lights the dead Lamp-post with a flame after dark — or simply when full night falls, if they wait — the gate opens.

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The taper touches the wick. The flame catches — and turns. Not orange. White, and cold, and far too bright, throwing your shadows long and wrong across the green. The fog rises off the mere uphill, against the wind, and pours into the light. The green is gone. The houses are gone. You stand on grey grass under a grey sky with no sun in it, and ahead, where the lamp-post stood, a dead iron post leans in a field of ash. From somewhere far below, a slow bell. And from somewhere nearer, the wet shuffle of many feet.*

They have crossed into **the Hollow**. The Pale Gate is behind them — the leaning dead post — and it will let them back out *if* they return to it. (Reassure the players, through the fiction, that the way back exists. Trapped-with-no-exit is not the threat here; the threat is the clock and the fights.)

The Hollow is a washed-out mirror of the moor: no sun, no color, fog instead of distance, everything a memory of Hollowmere gone grey. Darkvision and Light cantrips both work and both *matter* — light is the one thing the Hollow hates.

## Scene 4 — The Ashen Threshold (Combat 2, then Combat 3)

The mirror-green where the party arrives. A ruined echo of the Lamp-post leans in a field of ash; the burnt skeletons of the village houses stand around it. This is the Shepherd's doorstep, and his flock keeps it.

This is **two waves, back-to-back, with no rest between them.** It is the first real crucible. Run it as one continuous engagement: when wave 1 is nearly down, wave 2 comes out of the fog.

### Wave 1 — Threat 10

- **6 × Hollow Husk** (Threat 1 each).
- **2 × Pale Hound** (Threat 2 each).

The husks shamble in from the burnt houses; the hounds flank from the fog. A swarm fight — tests positioning and crowd control. The Priest's **Spirit Guardians** and the Mage's **Burning Hands / Thunderwave** shine against the clustered husks. Telegraph that fire and radiant *hurt these things more than steel does.*

### Wave 2 — Threat 14 (arrives as wave 1 breaks)

> *Read aloud, as the last husk of wave 1 falls:*
>
> *The fog doesn't thin — it thickens. Two shapes the size of men come through it, slow, dripping, their skin a grey gloss of cooling tallow, candle-stubs guttering where their eyes should be. Behind them, the pale lights kindle — two of them, bobbing, curious. And the hounds are back.*

- **2 × Wax-bound** (Threat 3 each) — slow, grabby; **weak to fire.**
- **2 × Pale Hound** (Threat 2 each).
- **2 × Lantern Wisp** (Threat 2 each) — drift, sting with cold light, hard to pin.

Wave 2 is harder and structurally nastier: the Wax-bound grapple and the wisps punish anyone who goes Down. This is where the party will burn resources. **Let wounded hounds and wisps flee** once the Wax-bound are dealt with — don't grind it to the last hit point.

### After the fight — the Stillwater Pool (respite)

West of the threshold, a still grey pool in a hollow of ash, perfectly mirror-flat, that the fog won't cross. It is a pocket of calm the Hollow can't reach.

- **A wounded villager soul** drifts here as a soul-lantern caught in the reeds. If freed (lift it out — no roll), it gratefully gives up its light: **one creature regains `2d8` HP**, then the soul-lantern dims to safe and the party can carry it (worth saving — see resolution). There is also a **mana draught** in a dead fisher's creel by the water — *drink as an action, regain 3 mana* (one use).
- **The Pool is a true rest.** A party that drinks deep and sleeps on its banks gets a **full rest** (all HP, all mana, all once-per-fight abilities, conditions cleared) — the only one available in the adventure.
- **The cost:** while they sleep, the Hollow feeds. **1d6 + 4 villagers are lost forever** — their lanterns gutter out down the Bone Road. **Tell the players this before they decide.** This is the central trade of the adventure: the rest that saves *you* costs the people you came to save.

Most parties will push on without the full rest, taking the free soul-heal and the mana draught and saving the pool for emergencies. That's the intended tension. Don't push them either way.

## Scene 5 — The Bone Road (movement & investigation)

A causeway of pale stones leads down from the threshold into the deep fog, between dead white trees. From the branches hang **soul-lanterns** — dozens of them, each a warm gold flicker behind glass, each with a faint face and a fainter voice. These are the people of Hollowmere.

This is the breather between crucibles: movement, investigation, and the clues that make the puzzle (Scene 6) and the boss (Scene 8) solvable. Walk them down it slowly.

- **The lanterns are the villagers.** Most are dim and far gone; a few still flicker bright enough to *speak*. The party can talk to them. (If the party rested at the pool, narrate the gaps — branches where a lantern hung this morning and hangs no longer.)

### What the souls tell you

Spread these across two or three talking lanterns (the map places **Old Tam** and **Lila** as named ones). Each is a villager who remembers being warm and alive — they are grateful, frightened, and helpful. They give:

- **Who the Shepherd is, and why.** *"Hesper carries us so kindly. He thinks the world out there only ever hurt us. He thinks this is mercy — to keep us, safe, in glass, forever. He's mad with kindness. You can't reason him out of it; we've tried for a hundred years of others he's taken."*
- **His weakness — his own flame.** *"He's not like us. We flicker, we remember, we change. His light burns cold and white and it never, ever changes. He keeps it in the Great Lantern in his hall. While that burns, you cannot end him — the flame holds him here, he just stands back up. Put out the Great Lantern, and he's only a tired old man who made a bad bargain. Then you can finish it."*
- **The Vault, and how to pass it.** *"The hall is sealed. The door only opens for the homes that were truly homes — light those, and only those. Don't light the cold ones. He hangs false lanterns of his own make among the hooks to fool you; they burn pale and they never change, like he does. Ours are warm. Ours remember."* — and the specific clues for the puzzle, below.
- **How to save them.** *"If you put him out, the lamps go free — and any of us you've kept lit, kept warm, come home with you. The dark ones... we're sorry. They're already gone."*

### Puzzle clues to plant here (don't flag them as clues)

Drop these naturally in the souls' dialogue. The party needs them in Scene 6:

- *"Light the homes. Leave the grave dark — a grave was never a home."* **(→ Grave: dark.)**
- *"We were never farmers — the moor gives no grain. The plough on the wall was rust before my gran was born. We fished the mere and cut the black peat."* **(→ Net: lit; Plough: dark.)**
- *"No cradle ever stayed lit without a hearth to warm it — light the hearth first, always, or the baby's lamp won't catch."* **(→ Cradle only after Hearth.)**
- *"The day-bell only rang once the looms went still and the work was done. Bell after loom; never before."* **(→ Bell only after Loom.)**

### The road's hazard (optional, light)

The fog itself is a mild hazard if the party dawdles or splits up: a PC who wanders off the pale stones into the fog alone must roll **Wilderness or Spirit, Hard (15)** to find the road again, or wastes time (advance the Guttering — narrate another lantern dying) and arrives Frightened. Keep this light; the road is mostly story, not a gauntlet.

## Scene 6 — The Lantern Vault (puzzle)

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The Bone Road ends at a round chamber walled in fog, and a great iron door without a handle, banded and sealed with cold white wax. Around the door hang seven empty iron hooks in an arc. On a stone shelf sit seven lanterns, each cold, each etched with a single mark: a **Hearth**, a **Loom**, a **Net**, a **Plough**, a **Cradle**, a **Bell**, a **Grave**. A low brazier of pale fire burns before the door. Carved over the lintel, in a neat, sad hand:*
>
> *"Only the homes that were homes may pass.*
> *Light them true, in their right order, and the hall will open.*
> *Light a false light, or a light out of turn, and begin again."*

This is a lights-on/lights-off logic puzzle with an ordering twist. The party hangs lanterns on the hooks and lights them at the brazier. **The door opens only when exactly the correct five lanterns burn, lit in a valid order, and the other two stay dark.**

### The solution (GM)

- **Light, and keep lit:** **Hearth, Loom, Net, Cradle, Bell** — the five true homes of a mere-fishing, peat-cutting, loom-working, child-raising, bell-keeping village.
- **Leave dark:** **Plough** (Hollowmere never farmed) and **Grave** (a grave was never a home).
- **Ordering constraints:**
  - **Hearth must be lit before Cradle.** (No cradle without a hearth to warm it.)
  - **Loom must be lit before Bell.** (The bell rang only after the looms went still.)
  - Net may be lit at any point among the valid ones. Hearth and Loom can be lit in either order relative to each other.

A valid order, for example: **Hearth → Loom → Net → Cradle → Bell.** (Any order satisfying "Hearth before Cradle" and "Loom before Bell," lighting exactly those five.)

### Failing a step

If the party **lights Plough or Grave**, or **lights Cradle before Hearth** or **Bell before Loom**:

> *The pale brazier flares. The lit lanterns gutter out, all at once. And the cold wax on the door bulges, drips, and stands up.*

- All lit lanterns **reset to dark** (start the sequence again).
- A **Wax-bound rises** from the door's wax (Threat 3) and attacks. It does **not** reset when the puzzle does — it's now a creature on the board. If the party keeps failing, they accumulate Wax-bound; this is the puzzle's teeth, and it flows naturally into Scene 7. (Cap it at +3 extra Wax-bound so it doesn't spiral; after that, just narrate flares without new monsters and lean on hints.)

### Don't let them get stuck

Per [../../rules/14-gm-guide.md](../../rules/14-gm-guide.md): a stuck party is a failure of the GM, not a punishment for the players. Escalating help:

1. **The clues from Scene 5** should be enough for most tables. If they didn't talk to the souls, a soul-lantern hangs in this room too and will answer questions.
2. If they're flailing, a successful **Investigation or Lore (Hard 15)** reveals one constraint per success ("the etching on the Plough is worn to nothing — this village never used it"; "the Cradle's glass is sooted as if it only ever sat by a fire").
3. If they're truly stuck or frustrated, the room's soul-lantern simply recites the answer in the souls' own voice — *"Hearth, then the loom, the net, the cradle, the bell. Leave the plough and the grave to the dark. Please — hurry."*

Solving the puzzle **without a single failure** is worth a small reward (see resolution — the souls "remember the kindness"). But the goal is to open the door and feel clever, not to suffer.

When solved, the white wax cracks and sloughs off the door, and it grinds open onto the Tallow Hall.

## Scene 7 — The Tallow Hall doors (Combat 4)

The door opens, but the Shepherd doesn't leave his hall unguarded. On the threshold wait his best — the armored remains of past would-be heroes who came through the gate over the centuries and *failed*, now hollow knights with cold lanterns for heads.

### Encounter — Threat 15 (the hardest fight before the boss)

- **2 × Hollow Knight** (Threat 5 each) — Sentinel; reach 2; **Lantern-Helm** gives them advantage while their head-lantern burns. Snuffing a helm (adjacent, Athletics or Arcana, Hard) strips the advantage — a tactical lever worth telegraphing.
- **1 × Wax-bound** (Threat 3) — plus **any Wax-bound the party spawned by failing the puzzle**, if those weren't killed in the Vault.
- **1 × Lantern Wisp** (Threat 2).

This fight is a wall of disciplined, durable enemies in a doorway — a deliberate contrast to the swarms before it. The Knights are too tough to ignore and too well-armored to brute-force; the party must **put out their lamps** (radiant, Light cantrip, an adjacent snuff action) to fight them on even terms. A good place for the Priest's **Channel Light** and the Mage's saved spells.

This is also the **last fight before the boss with no rest in between.** If the party hasn't used the Stillwater Pool, they'll be hurting. That's intended — but make sure at least one PC's healing is still online before you open the boss doors, or the boss becomes a TPK. If they're truly gutted, this is the moment a Knight can be narrated as "the last of them backs into the hall, and the doors begin to close" — giving the party a beat to heal before they push through. **Push, but don't execute them in the doorway.**

## Scene 8 — The Tallow Hall (Boss)

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The hall is vast and hung with light — hundreds, thousands of lanterns, on the walls, from the dark above, in drifts on the floor: every village he's ever taken, a hundred years of stolen warmth. At the far end, on an iron stand, burns the* **Great Lantern** *— white, cold, steady, a flame that has not flickered in a century. And before it stands a tall, thin man in a coat stitched from grey, a guttering candle where his face should be, a long hooked lantern-crook in his hands.*
>
> *He turns to you, and his voice is gentle, and unbearably sad.*
>
> *"You came all this way. For them. That's... that's lovely, truly. But you misunderstand. Out there they drown in the bog, they fever, they grow old and frightened and they die in the dark. Here, they never will. I keep them. I keep them safe. Let me keep you, too. Put down your steel. Step into the light. I promise you — it doesn't hurt at all."*

He means it. **The Pale Shepherd is not cruel; he is mad with mercy.** Play him sorrowful, patient, certain. He'll give the party every chance to surrender — and when they refuse, he sighs, and the fight begins, and he never stops sounding like he's doing them a kindness.

### Encounter — ~Threat 24 (200% boss)

- **The Pale Shepherd** (boss, Threat 14).
- **2 × Wax-bound** (Threat 3 each) — at his side; he made these himself, from the last village.
- **The Great Lantern** — environmental objective (no threat cost; it shapes the whole fight).
- **Call the Flock** — the Shepherd pulls husks up from the lantern-drifts as the fight runs (see his block).

### The Great Lantern (the win condition)

This is the fight's spine, like the Vesper Bell in *Ashvane*. **While the Great Lantern burns:**

- At the **start of each round**, the Pale Shepherd **regains 10 HP**.
- The Shepherd **cannot be reduced below 1 HP.** He is anchored to the flame; cut him down and he simply stands back up. **He cannot be killed while the Lantern burns.**

To win, the party must **put out the Great Lantern.** Ways to do it (reward whichever the players invent):

- **Snuff it directly.** Reach the stand and spend an action: a **Hard (15)** check — Athletics to wrench the great glass open and smother it, Arcana to unweave the cold flame, or Religion to rebuke it. The Shepherd is **Sentinel** and guards it jealously; getting there and getting the action off is the tactical problem of the fight.
- **Douse it.** A flask of the **Stillwater** (if a PC filled one at the pool — allow this if they thought of it; the still water is the one thing the Hollow's fog won't touch), or a big water effect, puts it out automatically with no roll. Reward the clever player who collected it.
- **Drown it in warmth.** A freed **soul-lantern** (from the Stillwater Pool, or any the party has been carrying) can be **thrown into the Great Lantern**: the warm, remembering light overwhelms the cold, steady one and snuffs it. Thematic, and it rewards saving souls. (One soul-lantern is enough.)

The instant the Great Lantern goes out:

> *The white flame dies. Every lantern in the hall flares warm and gold at once. And the tall man in grey staggers — the candle of his face guttering, suddenly only a wick — and for the first time in a hundred years, his light* flickers*.*

Now the Shepherd can be killed normally. He stops regenerating, drops to whatever HP he's at, and the regen/anchor abilities are gone.

### Tactics

- **The Shepherd opens with Snuff** on the most dangerous-looking caster (it halts healing — nasty), then keeps to his crook's 2-square reach, never leaving the Great Lantern undefended.
- He uses **Lantern-Gaze**: advantage against Frightened targets. The hall is full of frightening things; expect Frightened PCs. (This is why the pre-made party leans hard on anti-fear — the Dwarf Priest's Stone-Hearted, the Captain's-courage flask, Bless. Telegraph that staying un-Frightened is how you stay effective.)
- **Call the Flock:** once per round as a free action, the Shepherd reaches into the lantern-drifts and pulls up **1 Hollow Husk** (Threat 1) to defend the Lantern. This is his replenishing wave — it punishes a party that ignores the adds, and it's the dial you turn if the fight is too easy (more flock) or too hard (he's "too grieved to call them" this round).
- **When the Great Lantern goes out and he's brought low** (≤ 15 HP), he stops fighting and *begs* — not for his life, but to keep his purpose. He offers the party anything: their own dead returned, gold, the secret of the gate, *"just let me keep a few — just the old ones, the ones who'll only suffer out there —"*. Let the players choose how this ends. Killing him frees everyone. Sparing him is possible but he must be *unmade* (the gate sealed behind him, the Great Lantern destroyed) or he'll simply begin again with the next village.

### The Pale Shepherd — boss stat block

> **The Pale Shepherd** *(Hesper Loom, the Lamplighter)* — Tier 2 · Threat 14
> **HP** 95 · **Armor** 15 · **Mind** 15 · **Spirit** 16 · **Speed** 7
> *Attack:* **Shepherd's Crook** `+6 vs. Armor → 1d10 + 4`, reach 2 squares.
> *Attack:* **Snuff** `+6 vs. Spirit → 2d8` cold/necrotic, Range Near. On hit, the target's speed is halved and **they cannot be healed** until the end of their next turn ("the light goes thin").
> *Special — Sentinel.* Opportunity strikes are on for him; he uses them to guard the Great Lantern.
> *Special — Anchored to the Flame.* While the Great Lantern burns, at the start of each round he regains **10 HP**, and he **cannot be reduced below 1 HP**. Snuffing the Great Lantern ends both. (See "The Great Lantern.")
> *Special — Lantern-Gaze.* He has **advantage** on attacks against Frightened creatures.
> *Special — Call the Flock.* Once per round, as a free action, he raises **1 Hollow Husk** from the lantern-drifts (within Near range).
> *Immunities:* Charmed, Frightened, Poisoned. *Weakness:* takes **double damage from radiant**, and **+1d6** from fire.
> *Notes:* Sorrowful, gentle, utterly certain he is being kind. Will not flee — there is nowhere for him to go and nothing he wants but to keep his flock. When the Lantern is out and he is brought low, he begs to keep his purpose, not his life.

## Resolution

If the party puts out the Great Lantern and ends the Shepherd:

> *Read aloud:*
>
> *The grey man comes apart like a snuffed wick — a thread of smoke, a smell of tallow, gone. And the hall... the hall* sings*. Every warm lantern lifts at once, drifts toward the dead iron gate, and pours through it like a river of fireflies going home. The fog thins. Somewhere, impossibly, you hear a sun.*
>
> *You step back through the Pale Gate onto the green at Hollowmere — and the green is full of people. Blinking. Holding each other. Lighting the candles at the foot of the old lamp-post, one by one, until it blazes warm and orange and ordinary. Maren is in the middle of them, sobbing, being held by everyone at once.*

**How many come home** depends on the Guttering — how much the party dallied, and whether they took the full rest at the pool:

- **Sprinted, no full rest:** nearly all sixty. A clean, joyful save.
- **Took the full rest:** all but the `1d6 + 4` the Hollow ate while they slept — a bittersweet win, with empty houses and named griefs. (This is the cost the players chose. Honor it; don't soften it.)
- **Dawdled badly / failed the puzzle many times:** fewer still. Narrate the specific dead — Old Tam's lantern was one of the ones that guttered; Lila made it. Make the count *mean* something.

### Rewards

- **300 gold** total from the reeve of Bridghaven (150 advance + 150 on return), plus a bonus if most of Hollowmere lives.
- Each PC gains **1 level** (milestone leveling to level 4 — and at level 4 they each get an ability bump and a step to training bonus `+3`).
- **One magic item**, GM's choice — recommended: the **Lamplighter's Crook**, reforged from the Shepherd's: *a quarterstaff that sheds Light (as the cantrip) at will, and once per fight can be used to clear the Frightened condition from yourself and all allies in Near range.* A keeper for any party that walks into dark places.
- **The soul-lanterns the party saved and carried** are returned warm to their people — and the village of Hollowmere will keep a light burning for the party's names now, the way they once did for Hesper.
- **Information / hooks for a continuing campaign:**
  - The Hollow is still there, behind every dead lamp on a dark night. Hollowmere was not the first village Hesper took — that hall held *thousands* of lanterns, a hundred years of stolen villages. Some of those souls went home through the gate too. Some did not. *Where did they go?*
  - The gate is sealed *here*, but the Hollow has many doors.
  - Whatever Hesper *bargained with* in the fog a hundred years ago — the thing that owns the Hollow — is still owed a lamplighter.

### Failure modes

- **The party flees back through the gate empty-handed:** they live, the reeve pays only the advance, and Hollowmere is lost — sixty candles that never get lit again. A grim opening for a campaign of guilt and second chances.
- **The party is defeated:** the Shepherd does not kill them. He *keeps* them — gently, kindly, in glass. The survivors (any PC who escaped, or a new party) find six new soul-lanterns hanging on the Bone Road, still bright, still savable. A rescue hook, and a chance for a fallen PC to come home.
- **TPK with no escape:** the campaign's next party is hired to find out what happened to the *first* party that went to Hollowmere — and the whole adventure runs again, with the dead PCs as soul-lanterns to be freed.

## Bestiary — the Hollow's flock

Custom stat blocks for this adventure. All built to the apprentice-to-veteran targets in [../../rules/12-monsters.md](../../rules/12-monsters.md). **Radiant and fire are the universal weaknesses of the Hollow** — telegraph it.

> **Hollow Husk** — Tier 1 · Threat 1
> **HP** 12 · **Armor** 11 · **Mind** 7 · **Spirit** 8 · **Speed** 4
> *Attack:* **Grasping hands** `+3 vs. Armor → 1d6 + 1`.
> *Special:* Immune to Charmed / Frightened / Poisoned. Takes **+1d6 from radiant or fire.** When it drops to 0, its ember gutters out with a thin cold sigh.
> *Notes:* An emptied villager — eyes like cold wicks, moving like a sleepwalker. Slow, mindless, comes in swarms. Mercy to put down.

> **Pale Hound** — Tier 1 · Threat 2
> **HP** 13 · **Armor** 13 · **Mind** 10 · **Spirit** 9 · **Speed** 10
> *Attack:* **Bite** `+4 vs. Armor → 1d8 + 2`. On hit, a Tier-1-or-smaller target is knocked **Prone.**
> *Special:* **Pack Tactics** — advantage on attacks when an ally is adjacent to the same target. **Fog-step** — ignores difficult terrain while in fog. Shies from bright light (a creature shedding Light has half cover against it).
> *Notes:* Gaunt hounds of grey light, ribs like lantern-frames. Hit, retreat, circle. Flee when the pack is broken.

> **Lantern Wisp** — Tier 1 · Threat 2
> **HP** 10 · **Armor** 14 · **Mind** 12 · **Spirit** 12 · **Speed** fly 8
> *Attack:* **Cold Light** `+4 vs. Armor → 1d6` cold, Range Near.
> *Special:* **Flicker** — once per round as a free action, it dims; attacks against it have **disadvantage** until it next attacks. **Feed** — if it ends its turn adjacent to a Downed creature, that creature takes `1d6` and the wisp heals `1d6`. A creature shedding bright **Light** negates Flicker within Near.
> *Notes:* Bobbing pale lights that lure and sting. Cowardly; flees a losing fight. The Mage's Light cantrip ruins its day.

> **Wax-bound** — Tier 1 · Threat 3
> **HP** 22 · **Armor** 13 · **Mind** 9 · **Spirit** 10 · **Speed** 5
> *Attack:* **Molten grasp** `+5 vs. Armor → 1d8 + 2`. On hit, the target rolls **Agility vs. `+5`** or is **Restrained** by cooling wax (break free: Might, Hard).
> *Special:* Takes **+1d4 from fire** — but fire also *softens* it: a Wax-bound that took fire damage this round has its Restrain DC drop (break free becomes Easy). Immune to Charmed / Frightened / Poisoned. When killed, it hardens into a wax husk that makes its square **difficult terrain.**
> *Notes:* A villager half-dipped in tallow, candle-stubs for eyes, slow and dripping and terribly strong. Grabs and holds you for the wisps.

> **Hollow Knight** — Tier 2 · Threat 5
> **HP** 40 · **Armor** 16 · **Mind** 11 · **Spirit** 12 · **Speed** 6
> *Attack:* **Pale glaive** `+6 vs. Armor → 1d10 + 3`, reach 2 squares.
> *Special:* **Sentinel.** **Lantern-Helm** — a cold lantern burns where its head should be; while it burns, the Knight has **advantage** on attacks. An adjacent PC can **snuff the helm** with an action (Athletics or Arcana, Hard 15), or a hit of **radiant** snuffs it automatically — removing the advantage. Immune to Charmed / Frightened. Takes **+1d6 from radiant.**
> *Notes:* The armored remains of past would-be rescuers who came through the gate and lost. Disciplined, silent, holds the line. They guard; they do not pursue.

## GM cheat sheet

Print or pin this.

**The arc (escalating):** Combat 8 → *investigate* → Combat 10 → Combat 14 → *Bone Road* → *puzzle* → Combat 15 → Boss 24.

**Budget:** 12 (level 3 × 4 players). **Targets:** 10 / 15 / 20 (default 15).

**The Guttering (clock):** souls die when the party stalls. Full rest at the Stillwater Pool = **1d6+4 villagers lost.** Tell them before they choose.

**Universal weaknesses:** Hollow things take **+damage from radiant and fire.** The Shepherd takes **double radiant.** Light cantrips strip wisp Flicker and Knight advantage.

**Monster quick reference:**
- Hollow Husk — HP 12, Armor 11, `+3 → 1d6+1`. +1d6 radiant/fire. Swarm.
- Pale Hound — HP 13, Armor 13, `+4 → 1d8+2`, Prone. Pack Tactics. Speed 10.
- Lantern Wisp — HP 10, Armor 14, `+4 → 1d6` cold. Flicker. Feeds on Downed.
- Wax-bound — HP 22, Armor 13, `+5 → 1d8+2` + Restrain. +1d4 fire (and fire frees the grab).
- Hollow Knight — HP 40, Armor 16, `+6 → 1d10+3` reach 2. Sentinel. Lantern-Helm advantage (snuff it).
- **Pale Shepherd** — HP 95, Armor 15, Spirit 16. Crook `+6 → 1d10+4` reach 2; Snuff `+6 vs. Spirit → 2d8`, no-heal. Sentinel. **Anchored: while Great Lantern burns, +10 HP/round and can't drop below 1.** Lantern-Gaze (adv vs Frightened). Call the Flock (1 husk/round). Double radiant, +1d6 fire.

**Great Lantern:** put it out (Athletics/Arcana/Religion Hard 15 adjacent, OR Stillwater flask, OR throw a freed soul-lantern in) → Shepherd becomes mortal.

**Puzzle solution:** light **Hearth, Loom, Net, Cradle, Bell**; leave **Plough & Grave** dark. **Hearth before Cradle; Loom before Bell.** Wrong light → reset + a Wax-bound rises.

**NPC quick reference:**
- Maren — surviving apprentice, 14. Hid in the well. Knows the name (Hesper Loom) and the door.
- Soul-lanterns (Old Tam, Lila) — the villagers. Grateful, give the clues, can be saved.
- The Pale Shepherd — gentle, sorrowful, mad with mercy. Thinks glass is kinder than the world.
