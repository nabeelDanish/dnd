# The Pale Gate — Image-Generation Prompts

Copy-paste prompts for an image model (ChatGPT/DALL·E, Midjourney, etc.) — scenery, locations, NPCs, creatures, and the boss. *(No player-character art; bring your own party.)* Plain-description prompts work in ChatGPT; the `--ar`/`--v` flags are for Midjourney.

## Shared style (prepend or append to every prompt for a consistent look)

> *Painterly cinematic dark-fantasy concept art, melancholy folk-horror mood. Washed-out desaturated palette — grey peat moor, fog, bone-white dead trees, and a cold "wrong" white-blue flame — pierced by the one warmth in the world: golden, trapped soul-lantern light. Tallow sheen and cold black iron, atmospheric haze, high detail, fantasy film key-art.*

**Tips:** keep that style line identical across the set so they feel like one book. The whole adventure lives on a single contrast — **cold grey & wrong-white light vs. warm golden soul-light** — so name that contrast in every prompt. For Midjourney reuse one `--seed`; in ChatGPT say *"same style and palette as the last image."*

## The world (mortal side)

### 1 · Hollowmere, abandoned
> A tiny moor hamlet of about forty low stone houses around a green, sitting in a bowl of black peat bog under a heavy dusk sky. Every door stands open; a child's wooden horse lies in the empty lane; through a window, bowls of food gone grey with mould. Every hearth cold, every lamp gone. In the center of the green rises a tall, ornate **iron memorial lamp-post**, its great glass lantern dark, with **sixty unlit candles heaped at its base like grave-offerings**. Utterly silent, wrong, waiting. Wide establishing shot. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`

### 2 · The Pale Gate opens
> Night on the village green. A figure touches a taper to the dead iron lamp-post and its flame catches — **not orange, but cold white-blue, far too bright**, throwing long wrong shadows. Fog rises off the black mere *uphill, against the wind,* and pours into the white light; the village dissolves into grey at the edges of the glow. A door between worlds, opening. Eerie, vertiginous, beautiful and wrong. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`

### 3 · The villagers walk into the light *(backstory / mood)*
> Seen from the lip of a well at night: the whole population of a moor village — men, women, children — walking calmly, **smiling**, in a line into the cold white glow of a tall iron lamp-post, vanishing one by one. A tall, thin grey figure with a lantern on a long crook watches them go. Dreamlike horror, soft and terrible, fog and frost. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`

## The Hollow (the grey mirror-world)

### 4 · The Ashen Threshold
> A washed-out grey mirror of a moor village: no sun, a colorless sky, fog instead of distance. The **burnt skeletons of stone houses** stand around a ruined, leaning dead iron lamp-post in a field of ash. Shambling grey husks and gaunt hounds of pale light move through the fog. Desolate, monochrome, the smell of cold ash made visible. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`

### 5 · The Bone Road
> A causeway of pale flat stones leading down into deep fog between **dead, bone-white trees**. From the bare branches hang **dozens of glass soul-lanterns**, each a warm gold flicker with a faint sorrowful human face inside — the trapped souls of a village. The single thread of warmth and color in an endless grey. Haunting, mournful, lovely. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`

### 6 · The Stillwater Pool
> A perfectly mirror-flat grey pool in a hollow of ash, so still it doubles the colorless sky; the fog will not cross its edge. A single **golden soul-lantern caught among dead reeds** at the water's edge, glowing warm. A pocket of eerie calm the grey world cannot reach. *[style]* `--ar 3:2 --v 6`

### 7 · The Lantern Vault
> A round chamber walled in fog. A great handleless **iron door sealed with cold white wax**, an arc of seven empty iron hooks above it. On a stone shelf, seven cold lanterns each etched with a single symbol — a hearth, a loom, a net, a plough, a cradle, a bell, a grave. A low **brazier of pale white fire** burns before the door. A sad, neat inscription carved over the lintel. A puzzle-shrine, patient and cold. *[style]* `--ar 3:2 --v 6`

### 8 · The Tallow Hall *(boss arena)*
> A vast cathedral-like hall hung with **thousands upon thousands of lanterns** — on the walls, hanging from the unseen dark above, drifted across the floor — a hundred years of stolen warmth, all glowing faint gold. At the far end, on a black iron stand, burns the **Great Lantern**: a single cold, steady, white flame that has not flickered in a century. Overwhelming, beautiful, and grief-soaked. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`

## NPCs, creatures & the boss

### 9 · The Pale Shepherd *(boss — Hesper Loom)*
> A tall, unnaturally thin figure in a long coat **stitched together from scraps of grey cloth**, standing in a hall of lanterns. Where his face should be, a single **guttering candle-flame**, wax running like tears. In his hands, a long iron **lantern-crook**. His posture is gentle, sorrowful, patient — a monster utterly convinced he is being kind. Beside him, cold white light. Quietly terrifying. *[style]* `--ar 2:3 --v 6`

### 10 · A soul-lantern (close)
> Extreme close-up of a single ornate glass lantern hanging from a bone-white branch, a **warm golden flame inside, and within the flame the faint, gentle, frightened face of a villager** — an old man, eyes pleading. The glass beaded with grey mist. Intimate, mournful, the human cost in one image. *[style]* `--ar 1:1 --v 6`

### 11 · Maren, the survivor *(NPC)*
> Portrait of a thin, hollow-eyed fourteen-year-old girl in a moor-village shawl, hiding in a dark root-cellar lit by a single stub of candle, clutching her knees, equal parts terror and stubborn hope on her dirt-smudged face. Three weeks alone. Sympathetic, vulnerable, human. *[style]* `--ar 2:3 --v 6`

### 12 · The Hollow's flock *(creatures — render as a set or individually)*
> A bestiary line-up of grey folk-horror creatures from a faded mirror-world: a slow **Hollow Husk** (an emptied villager, eyes like cold wicks); a gaunt **Pale Hound** of grey light with ribs like a lantern-frame; a bobbing **Lantern Wisp** of cold pale light; a **Wax-bound** (a villager half-dipped in dripping tallow, candle-stubs for eyes); and an armored **Hollow Knight** with a cold lantern burning where its head should be. Desaturated, eerie, each flinching from light. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`

## Resolution

### 13 · The lights come home
> A moor village green at dawn, suddenly **full of blinking, weeping people** holding each other, lighting the sixty candles at the foot of a tall iron lamp-post one by one until it blazes **warm orange and ordinary**. The grey fog burning off, the first real color returning to the world. Relief, warmth, hard-won. *[style]* `--ar 16:9 --v 6`
